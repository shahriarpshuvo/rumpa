## When Is the Gestational Diabetes Test Done? (Standard Timing)
- Targets long-tail: "what week is the gestational diabetes test done"
- Universal screening between 24 and 28 weeks for average-risk pregnancies. `<Cite n="1" />` (ACOG, USPSTF, NICE NG3)
- Why this window: placental hormone resistance peaks here, so the test catches GD when it matters.

## OGTT vs Glucose Challenge, What Your Doctor Actually Orders
- Targets secondary kw: "OGTT pregnancy" + "glucose tolerance test weeks"
- UK / NICE NG3: 75g 2-hour OGTT (fasting, then 1-hr, then 2-hr). One-step. `<Cite n="2" />`
- US two-step (most common): non-fasting 50g glucose challenge → if abnormal, 100g 3-hour OGTT. `<Cite n="3" />`
- US one-step (ADA-preferred): 75g 2-hour OGTT.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Same disease, different protocols, similar diagnostic accuracy.

## What the Test Day Looks Like (UK + US)
- Fasting 8-10 hours night before (water only).
- Bring snacks for after.
- Drink the glucose load in 5 min; sit still (no walking).
- 1-hr and 2-hr draws.
- Expect 2-3 hours total at the clinic.
- `<Callout type="note">` Mild nausea is common; tell staff if you vomit, the test usually has to repeat.

## Early GD Screening for High-Risk Pregnancies
- Targets secondary kw: "early GD screening high risk"
- Long-tail in prose: "Do I need an early GD test with PCOS?"
- ADA 2026 Ch 15 recommends screening before 15 weeks if risk factors present: PCOS, BMI ≥30, previous GD, family history, prior macrosomic baby, South Asian/African/Hispanic ancestry. `<Cite n="4" />`
- NICE NG3: early OGTT at booking + repeat at 24-28 weeks if first is normal.
- `<Callout type="warning">` PCOS doubles GD risk. If you have PCOS, ask for early screening at your booking appointment. (Link to spoke [PCOS and GD link](/blog/pcos-and-gd-link).)

## What Your Numbers Mean (Result Cutoffs)
- NICE NG3 diagnostic cutoffs (75g OGTT): fasting ≥5.6 mmol/L OR 2-hr ≥7.8 mmol/L.
- ADA / IADPSG (one-step 75g): fasting ≥92 mg/dL, 1-hr ≥180, 2-hr ≥153.
- Two-step (Carpenter-Coustan, 100g, 3-hr): 2 or more values met = diagnosis.

## What Happens If You're Diagnosed
- 1-2 week diet + monitoring trial first (linking up to cornerstone [Gestational diabetes diet plan](/blog/gestational-diabetes-diet-plan)).
- Glucose meter, finger-prick 4x daily (fasting + 1-hr post each meal).
- Dietitian + diabetes midwife referral (NHS) or maternal-fetal medicine (US).

## What This Means for You
- Most pregnancies get tested at 24-28 weeks. If you have PCOS, prior GD, or high BMI, ask about early screening. Primary kw "gestational diabetes test when" in last 200 words.

## What PCOS Actually Does to Fertility ← targets: "PCOS conception odds"
- Explain the ovulation problem in plain English: irregular or absent ovulation is the main fertility barrier, not the cysts themselves.
- Around 1 in 10 women in the UK have PCOS per NHS; many conceive without help.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` PCOS is a fertility delay, not a fertility verdict.

## What Are the Chances of Getting Pregnant With PCOS?
- Natural rates: roughly 70% of women with PCOS experience difficulty ovulating regularly, but a meaningful minority conceive naturally with no intervention.
- With first-line treatment (lifestyle + letrozole/clomiphene), live-birth rates approach those of unaffected women across cumulative cycles.
- Long-tail answered in prose: "what are the chances of getting pregnant with PCOS".

## Step One: The Lifestyle Foundation
- Evidence-based, not weight-loss-shaming: small reductions in body weight (5-10%) restore ovulation for many; the goal is metabolic, not cosmetic.

## NHS vs US Pathway for PCOS Fertility Care
- **UK / NHS side:** GP first, BMI thresholds for NHS-funded IVF vary by ICB, NICE NG257 defines infertility (12 months trying or 6 cycles donor insemination), letrozole used off-label but widely.
- **US side:** OB-GYN or REI direct, insurance varies by state, faster access to ovulation induction and IUI.

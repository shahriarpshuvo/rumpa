## How Strong Is the PCOS and Gestational Diabetes Connection? ← targets: "PCOS pregnancy risk"
- Key points:
  - Systematic reviews and meta-analyses put GDM risk at roughly 2-3x baseline for women with PCOS, independent of BMI.
  - EGOI-PCOS 2025 position statement confirms PCOS as an independent GDM risk factor.
  - Quantify: roughly 4-10% baseline GDM rate in general population, vs higher rates reported across PCOS cohorts.
  - `<Cite n="1" />` for systematic review.

## Why PCOS Predisposes to GD, the Biology ← targets: "insulin resistance pregnancy"
- Key points:
  - Insulin resistance is the metabolic engine of both conditions.
  - In pregnancy, placental hormones add a second insulin-resistance load on top of the PCOS baseline.
  - Hyperandrogenism and low SHBG independently associate with higher GDM risk.
  - Lean PCOS is not protective; insulin resistance can be present without obesity.
  - `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` on lean PCOS and GD risk.

## Who Is Most at Risk Within the PCOS Group ← targets: "PCOS pregnancy risk"
- Key points:
  - Higher pre-pregnancy BMI raises risk further.
  - Pre-conception impaired glucose tolerance, low SHBG, high fasting insulin and HOMA-IR.
  - Age over 35, South Asian or East Asian ethnicity.
  - Prior GDM.
  - Answer long-tail: "does PCOS always lead to gestational diabetes?" No. It raises risk; it does not guarantee it.

## Pre-Conception Screening, What I Recommend ← targets: "PCOS GD prevention strategies"
- Key points:
  - The 2023 International PCOS guideline recommends OGTT (or fasting glucose or HbA1c) before conception or fertility treatment.
  - If not done pre-conception, OGTT before 20 weeks gestation, and again at 24-28 weeks.
  - Pre-conception weight optimisation where relevant, but without before/after framing.
  - Folic acid (high-dose 5 mg if BMI over 30), vitamin D.
  - Internal link to pre-conception-health-checklist.

## Prevention Strategies That Have Evidence ← targets: "PCOS GD prevention strategies"
- Key points:
  - Pre-conception metformin in selected cases (discuss with your team; not a blanket recommendation).
  - Inositol: emerging evidence for GD risk reduction in PCOS; not yet a guideline recommendation.
  - Dietary pattern: lower glycaemic load, adequate protein, regular meals.
  - Movement: 150 minutes moderate activity per week per WHO/NICE.
  - Sleep and stress management, often skipped in advice columns, both affect insulin sensitivity.
  - Answer long-tail: "how to reduce GD risk if you have PCOS" answered here.
  - `<Callout type="warning">` on not starting any medication without clinician guidance.

## During Pregnancy, the Monitoring Plan ← targets: long-tail
- Key points:
  - Early OGTT (before 20 weeks) often offered, especially with prior GDM or BMI over 30.
  - Standard 24-28 week OGTT.
  - Self-monitoring of blood glucose if GD is diagnosed.
  - Multidisciplinary team: OB-GYN, diabetes midwife, dietitian.
  - NHS pathway note plus US ACOG note in side boxes.

## When PCOS and GD Both Happen, What to Know ← targets: bridge to GD cluster
- Key points:
  - Studies suggest higher rates of pregnancy-induced hypertension and certain neonatal outcomes when both PCOS and GDM are present.
  - This is not a reason to panic; it is a reason to be monitored attentively.
  - Bridge to gestational-diabetes-diet-plan cornerstone and to gd-bedtime-snacks for tactical content.
  - `<Callout type="note">` reassuring that well-monitored, well-controlled GD outcomes are good.

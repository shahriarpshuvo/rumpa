## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- If you have been trying for a year (or 6 months if you are 36 or over) and your GP is the next stop, knowing the fertility testing UK NHS pathway in advance changes the appointment from confusing to useful. The NHS does test, it tests in a set order, and what you get depends partly on your age and partly on your postcode. Here is what to expect, and what to ask for.

## When the NHS starts fertility testing ← targets: "what fertility tests does the NHS offer"
- The trigger: 12 months trying under 36, 6 months trying if 36 or over, or sooner with a known cause (PCOS, endometriosis, prior cancer treatment, irregular cycles) `<Cite n="1" />`.
- Start with the GP, both partners assessed.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "Book the GP appointment as a couple where possible, the workup is for both of you."

## NHS fertility blood tests, what they actually check ← targets: "NHS fertility blood tests"
- Day 21 progesterone (or 7 days before the next period) to confirm ovulation.
- FSH, LH, oestradiol on day 2-5 in some pathways.
- AMH for ovarian reserve when IVF is being considered.
- TSH, prolactin, rubella and chlamydia screen.
- `<Callout type="note">` Patients ask me about AMH first. NHS will usually only do it once a referral to secondary care is in motion.

## Tests for your partner ← targets: secondary
- Semen analysis arranged through the GP via the local andrology lab.
- Repeat after 3 months if the first is abnormal.
- Link to [When Should Your Partner Get a Sperm Analysis?](/blog/sperm-analysis-partner-test).

## Imaging and the tubal check ← targets: secondary
- Pelvic ultrasound at the GP or secondary care.
- HSG (X-ray dye test) or HyCoSy (ultrasound contrast) for the tubes.
- Laparoscopy if endometriosis is suspected.
- Tests NICE does not recommend routinely: post-coital test, endometrial biopsy, routine prolactin without symptoms `<Cite n="2" />`.

## NICE fertility guidance and what changed ← targets: "NICE fertility guidance"
- NICE NG257 (2024) replaced CG156, updated investigations and IVF age thresholds.
- Prognosis-based decisions, not just calendar try-time.
- Link to [Unexplained Infertility, Your Next Steps](/blog/unexplained-infertility-next-steps).

## NHS IVF eligibility and waiting times ← targets: "NHS IVF eligibility"
- Funded cycles vary by Integrated Care Board (ICB). NICE recommends up to 3 full cycles for under-40s and 1 for 40-42, but most ICBs fund fewer `<Cite n="3" />`.
- Common criteria: BMI usually 19-30, non-smoker, no living children in either partner, partner has no children from previous relationship, female age cap typically 40 or 42.
- How long is the NHS fertility waiting list, honest answer: 12-18 months to first treatment in many regions, longer in some.

## What to do if you do not qualify for NHS IVF ← bridges to related Rumpa post
- Self-funded private investigation, second opinion, or top-up cycles.
- Link to [IVF Cost in the UK, NHS vs Private](/blog/ivf-cost-uk).

## What This Means for You
- The fertility testing UK NHS pathway is structured and free at the point of use, but it moves at NHS pace. Knowing the right tests to expect at each stage means you ask the right questions and you do not waste a year on tests that should have been parallel.

## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- The question of when to see a fertility specialist is rarely about a single number on the calendar; it is about the right doctor at the right time, in the right country's pathway. UK and US patients face very different routes to the same answer. This guide walks you through both, plus the personal triggers that should accelerate the decision wherever you live.

## The Short Answer ← targets: "when to see fertility specialist"
- Under 35: see your GP/PCP at 12 months of trying without success
- 35-39: at 6 months
- 40+: at the start of trying, do not wait
- Earlier if: known cause, irregular cycles, prior pelvic surgery, male-factor history, recurrent loss
- Long-tail in prose: "when is the right time to see a fertility doctor"
- `<Cite n="1" />` ASRM Definition of Infertility (2023)

## What "Specialist" Actually Means ← targets: "infertility evaluation"
- GP/PCP: triage and basic workup
- OB-GYN: tests, often initial treatment
- Reproductive Endocrinologist (REI): IVF, complex cases
- UK consultant gynaecologist with fertility sub-specialty
- Why the title matters for the test set you will get

## How the NHS Fertility Referral Pathway Works ← targets: "NHS fertility referral"
- Start with GP: history, BMI, smoking, alcohol, baseline bloods, semen analysis
- GP completes initial workup before tertiary referral
- Long-tail in prose: "do I need a GP referral to see a fertility specialist UK"
- Postcode lottery: ICB criteria vary on age caps, BMI, prior children, partner status
- Tertiary referral to NHS fertility clinic for IVF assessment
- Link to nhs-fertility-testing spoke
- `<Callout type="note">` UK private route bypasses GP but you still benefit from one for record continuity

## How the US Fertility Specialist Pathway Works ← targets: "US fertility specialist"
- OB-GYN as first stop; many run a complete workup before REI referral
- Direct access to REI in most states (no PCP referral needed for self-pay)
- Insurance plan rules vary on diagnostic vs treatment coverage
- State mandates (e.g. California SB 729 from 2026 expanding IVF coverage)
- Link to us-fertility-testing-what-to-ask spoke
- `<Cite n="2" />` RESOLVE state coverage map

## NHS vs US Pathway
- Two-column callout:
  - **UK / NHS:** GP gatekeeper, 12-month rule (or 6 months at 35+), free initial workup, BMI and age caps for IVF funding, postcode variation
  - **US:** Direct OB-GYN or REI access, insurance defines coverage, faster start, costs front-loaded, state mandates vary
- Both pathways: partner is part of the workup from day one

## Red Flags That Override the Calendar
- Irregular or absent cycles (PCOS suspected)
- Very painful or heavy periods (endometriosis suspected)
- Prior chemotherapy, pelvic surgery, or pelvic infection
- Male partner with known fertility history, prior cancer treatment, or recent illness
- Recurrent miscarriage (2+ losses)
- Known low ovarian reserve in a family member with early menopause
- Long-tail in prose: examples reinforce "right time to see a fertility doctor"
- Link to sperm-analysis-partner-test and unexplained-infertility-next-steps spokes
- `<Callout type="warning">` These triggers warrant a GP/PCP visit now, regardless of how long you have been trying

## The Age 35+ Conversation, Honestly
- Fertility decline is gradual, not a cliff
- ACOG 2025 anticipatory counselling guidance
- Why 6 months matters more than 12 at this age
- AMH and antral follicle count as planning data
- South Asian diaspora context: later marriage timing often shifts the first specialist conversation closer to 35-37; that does not mean panic, it means prioritise the appointment
- Link to ttc-after-35 spoke

## What to Expect at Your First Specialist Appointment ← targets: "infertility evaluation"
- History: cycle pattern, prior pregnancies, surgeries, family history
- Examination: pelvic exam, often a baseline ultrasound
- Bloods: day-3 hormones, AMH, TSH, prolactin, rubella, vit D
- Imaging: HSG for tubal patency, sometimes saline sonogram
- Partner: semen analysis
- Typical 4-8 week investigation window

## How to Prepare for the Visit
- 3-cycle period diary
- All previous test results in one folder
- List of medications and supplements (including dose)
- Partner's history written down
- 3-5 questions written in advance
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Patients who come prepared with this information get a better workup faster, in my experience

## When Telemedicine Fits (and When It Does Not)
- Pre-visit triage, second opinions, results review work well online
- Examination, HSG, and ultrasound need in-person

## What This Means for You
- Knowing when to see a fertility specialist is half timing, half pathway. If you are at the 12-month (or 6-month, at 35+) mark, or any red flag applies, take the next step. If you are in the UK book your GP; if you are in the US call your OB-GYN or an REI directly. A planned conversation now prevents a panicked one in six months. `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`

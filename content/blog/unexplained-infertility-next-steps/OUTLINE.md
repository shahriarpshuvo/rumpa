## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- An "unexplained" label after months of tests can feel like the worst answer. It is not. Knowing the unexplained infertility next steps that match your age, try-time and prognosis is what turns the diagnosis into a plan.

## What does unexplained infertility actually mean? ← targets: "what does unexplained infertility actually mean"
- A diagnosis of exclusion, given when the standard workup is normal: ovulation confirmed, tubes open, semen analysis normal, ovarian reserve adequate.
- Around 1 in 4 couples investigated for infertility end up here `<Cite n="1" />`.
- It does not mean "nothing is wrong", it means current tests cannot find it.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "Unexplained means undetected, not untreatable."

## How long to try after an unexplained infertility diagnosis ← targets: "how long to try after unexplained infertility diagnosis"
- Age is the biggest single factor.
- Under 35 with under 2 years of trying, expectant management with timed intercourse for 6-12 months is still reasonable.
- 35-37, do not wait beyond 6 months once the workup is clean.
- 38+, escalate now.
- Link to [Trying to Conceive After 35, Explained](/blog/ttc-after-35).

## Unexplained infertility treatment options, ranked ← targets: "unexplained infertility treatment options"
- Three options on the table: expectant management, IUI with ovarian stimulation, IVF.
- Prognosis-based decision making (Hunault model and successors), not one-size-fits-all `<Cite n="2" />`.
- `<Callout type="note">` Patients who come to me asking "should we just go to IVF", I help them weigh prognosis, cost, time and emotional load.

## IUI for unexplained infertility, who it helps and who it does not ← targets: "IUI for unexplained infertility"
- IUI plus ovarian stimulation gives meaningful live-birth gains for women under 38 with good prognosis `<Cite n="3" />`.
- Typical plan: 3-4 cycles, then reassess.
- Recent ESHRE position supports IUI first for good-prognosis under-38s.
- Costs in UK private and US contexts.

## IVF after unexplained diagnosis ← targets: "IVF after unexplained diagnosis"
- NICE NG257 currently sets IVF as the recommended treatment after 2 years of trying with unexplained infertility, although guidance is under active review `<Cite n="4" />`.
- IVF has the highest per-cycle live-birth rate but real cost, access and emotional cost.
- Link to [IUI vs IVF, Which Is Right for You?](/blog/iui-vs-ivf).

## UK and US pathways, what to actually do next ← bridges to related Rumpa post
- UK: GP referral after 1 year (6 months if 36+), NHS funded IVF varies by ICB.
- US: self-refer to an REI, insurance check first.
- Link to [NHS Fertility Testing, What You Get and When](/blog/nhs-fertility-testing) and [Fertility Testing in the US, What to Ask](/blog/us-fertility-testing-what-to-ask).

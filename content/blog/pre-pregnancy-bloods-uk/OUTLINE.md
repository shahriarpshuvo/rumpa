## What the NHS Routinely Offers Pre-Conception ← targets: "NHS pre-conception bloods"
- Key points:
  - Outside of antenatal booking, the NHS does not run a standard "pre-pregnancy panel".
  - GPs will offer relevant tests based on history: thyroid, ferritin, vitamin D, blood group if unknown.
  - Cervical screening review.
  - The NHS pathway is largely "we'll test if there's a reason"; private clinics offer panels by default.
  - `<Cite n="1" />` to NHS pre-pregnancy guidance.

## Bloods Worth Asking Your GP About ← targets: "what blood tests should I have before pregnancy in the UK"
- Key points:
  - Full blood count (FBC) for anaemia.
  - Ferritin (iron stores): often low in menstruating women.
  - Vitamin D: NHS recommends supplementation in pregnancy; many start low.
  - Blood group and rhesus status if unknown.
  - HbA1c if any diabetes risk factors.
  - Long-tail in prose: "Which pre-pregnancy bloods does the NHS cover?"

## Thyroid Function, Why It Matters ← targets: "thyroid TSH pre-conception"
- Key points:
  - TSH and free T4 to screen for hypothyroidism or subclinical hypothyroidism.
  - NICE NG145 and Endocrine Society guidance on thyroid pre-conception and in pregnancy.
  - Why an "in range" TSH may still need adjustment when planning pregnancy.
  - `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` on the lower TSH target in early pregnancy.

## Rubella, Chickenpox, and Other Immunity Checks ← targets: "rubella immunity test pregnancy"
- Key points:
  - Rubella immunity should be confirmed pre-pregnancy. If not immune, MMR can be given before conception (avoid pregnancy for 4 weeks after).
  - Varicella (chickenpox) immunity for women without prior infection or vaccination.
  - Hepatitis B status if at risk.
  - HIV and syphilis are routinely offered at booking; some women prefer to do these pre-conception.

## PCOS, Thyroid Disease, and Recurrent Miscarriage, Extra Tests ← targets: secondary
- Key points:
  - PCOS / suspected PCOS: OGTT or fasting glucose plus HbA1c per the 2023 International PCOS Guideline.
  - Recurrent miscarriage (2+): antiphospholipid syndrome screen, thyroid antibodies, karyotype as indicated.
  - Suspected endometriosis or pelvic pathology may prompt different workup.
  - Internal link to pcos-and-gd-link.

## Carrier and Genetic Screening, the Quiet Gap ← targets: long-tail
- Key points:
  - NHS offers sickle cell and thalassaemia screening at booking; can be requested pre-pregnancy if you are concerned about carrier status.
  - Expanded carrier screening (cystic fibrosis, spinal muscular atrophy) is not standard NHS pre-conception, but available privately. ACOG recommends; NHS lags.
  - When ethnicity, family history, or consanguinity raise the case for earlier screening.

## NHS vs Private, How to Decide ← targets: bridge
- Key points:
  - When NHS is enough: low-risk, no concerning history, regular cycles, no chronic conditions.
  - When private adds value: PCOS, thyroid, age 35+, prior pregnancy loss, family history of inherited conditions.
  - Cost ballpark for a private pre-conception panel in the UK.

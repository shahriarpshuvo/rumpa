## The Reliable Signs, Ranked ← targets: "signs of ovulation"
- Tier 1 (high reliability): LH surge on a urine kit, fertile cervical mucus (egg-white, stretchy), biphasic BBT pattern across a cycle.
- Tier 2 (supportive): cervix position changes, mittelschmerz (one-sided pelvic twinge), libido lift.
- Tier 3 (low specificity, often misread): breast tenderness, bloating, mood shifts.
- Long-tail answered in prose: "what does ovulation feel like".
- `<Cite n="1" />` PMC review of physiological signs observable by women.

## Cervical Mucus, the Underrated MVP ← targets: "cervical mucus changes"
- Dry → sticky → creamy → watery → egg-white. Egg-white mucus is the green light.
- How to check (toilet paper or clean finger), what is normal, what is not.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Cervical mucus peak aligns with the LH surge ~78% of the time within ±1 day.

## The LH Surge and How Kits Read It ← targets: "LH surge"
- How OPKs work, when to test (afternoon, consistent time), how to read digital vs line tests.
- Why a positive test means ovulation typically within 24-36 hours, not immediately.
- PCOS caveat: false-positive LH patterns are common, OPKs are less reliable.

## Basal Body Temperature, the Retrospective Confirmer ← targets: "basal body temperature"
- BBT confirms ovulation already happened, it does not predict it.
- How to take it accurately (same time, before getting up, oral or vaginal thermometer).
- The biphasic pattern, the temperature shift (0.2-0.5°C), and the luteal phase.
- `<Cite n="2" />` StatPearls BBT chapter.
- Internal link: bbt-accuracy-ttc.

## When Your Signs Contradict
- LH positive but no BBT shift: possible LUF (luteinised unruptured follicle) or simply a late ovulation.
- Egg-white mucus without a temperature shift: oestrogen rise without ovulation, common in PCOS.
- When to bring 2-3 charted cycles to a clinician.
- Internal link: how-to-know-if-you-have-pcos for irregular cycles.

## NHS vs US Pathway for Ovulation Investigation
- **UK / NHS:** GP can order day-21 progesterone (luteal phase check) after 6-12 months TTC depending on age and history; pelvic ultrasound and AMH usually require referral.
- **US:** OB-GYN can typically order the same panel sooner; insurance varies.

## How PCOS Affects Fertility (Quick Recap) ← "what is the first-line treatment for PCOS infertility"
- PCOS blocks fertility primarily through anovulation: cycles without releasing an egg.
- Insulin resistance underlies many cases (now reframed as PMOS = Polycystic Metabolic-Ovarian Syndrome).
- Egg quality is generally normal in PCOS; the issue is getting the egg out reliably.
- Most women with PCOS will conceive with the right pathway.
- Internal link: `can-you-get-pregnant-with-pcos`, `how-to-know-if-you-have-pcos`.
- `<Cite n="1" />` 2023 International PCOS Guideline.

## Step 1: Lifestyle and Medical Optimisation (the Foundation)
- Even modest weight change (5-10%) restores ovulation in many women with PCOS and elevated BMI.
- Resistance training + 150 min/wk moderate activity improves insulin sensitivity.
- Low-glycaemic / Mediterranean-pattern eating supports cycle regularity.
- Sleep, stress, and shift-work matter (HPO axis).
- This is not "fix yourself first" gatekeeping; it is the foundation that makes every subsequent treatment work better.
- Internal link: `pcos-diet-to-conceive`, `pcos-insulin-resistance-fertility`.
- `<Cite n="2" />` 2023 PCOS Guideline lifestyle recommendations.

## Step 2: Inositol and Metformin (Insulin-Sensitising) ← "metformin for fertility"
- Myo-inositol (often 2 g twice daily, often with D-chiro-inositol in 40:1 ratio): improves cycle regularity and ovulation in PCOS; well-tolerated.
- Metformin: improves ovulation, especially in BMI > 25 / insulin resistance / impaired glucose tolerance; can support conception alongside letrozole.
- Both are adjuncts, not standalone "fertility drugs".
- Internal link: `inositol-vs-metformin-pcos`.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "In my clinic, women often ask whether to pick inositol or metformin. I usually start inositol first for mild cases and use metformin when there is clear insulin resistance on bloods or a higher BMI."
- `<Cite n="3" />` 2023 PCOS Guideline metformin recommendations.

## Step 3: Letrozole (First-Line Ovulation Induction) ← "letrozole for PCOS"
- Letrozole is now the first-line drug for PCOS ovulation induction per the 2023 International Guideline, ASRM, Endocrine Society, and ESHRE.
- It beat clomid in the PPCOS-II trial (NEJM 2014): higher ovulation, higher live birth.
- Typical regimen: 2.5-7.5 mg daily on cycle days 2-6 (titrated up over cycles).
- Cycle monitoring with follicle scans where access permits.
- Risks: multiples (mostly twins) ~3-5%, mild hot flushes.
- Internal link: `letrozole-vs-clomid-pcos`, `ovulation-induction-what-to-expect`.
- `<Cite n="4" />` NEJM Legro PPCOS-II 2014; `<Cite n="5" />` 2023 PCOS Guideline ovulation induction.

## Step 4: Clomiphene Citrate (Second-Line, Still Useful)
- Was the first-line for decades; now second-line in most guidelines but still widely used (cost, access).
- Higher rate of thin endometrial lining; more multiples than letrozole.
- Common UK NHS prescribing pattern where letrozole access is limited.
- Internal link: `letrozole-vs-clomid-pcos`.
- `<Cite n="4" />` NEJM Legro 2014.

## Step 5: Gonadotropin Injections (FSH/LH) and Ovarian Drilling
- FSH injections (e.g. follitropin): used when oral agents fail. Higher cost, requires monitoring, higher multiples risk.
- Laparoscopic ovarian drilling: targeted surgical option, helpful for clomid/letrozole-resistant PCOS, restores ovulation in a meaningful subset.
- Both typically delivered through a fertility clinic, not GP-led.
- `<Cite n="2" />` 2023 PCOS Guideline second-line interventions.

## Step 6: IUI vs IVF in PCOS ← "IVF with PCOS" / "when does PCOS need IVF"
- IUI: usually combined with ovulation induction; helpful where there is an additional mild factor (male, cervical, anatomical).
- IVF indications in PCOS: failure of first- and second-line induction, additional factors (tubal, severe male), advanced age, time pressure.
- IVF in PCOS: higher egg yield, but higher OHSS risk; modern antagonist protocols + GnRH agonist trigger + freeze-all strategies make IVF safer than 10 years ago.
- Live-birth rates with PCOS in IVF are comparable to or better than non-PCOS in many series.
- Internal link: `ivf-with-pcos-success`, `iui-vs-ivf`, `when-to-consider-ivf-after-ttc`.
- `<Cite n="6" />` ESHRE OHSS prevention.

## Special Considerations: Lean PCOS, Older Reproductive Age, GD Risk
- Lean PCOS: weight loss isn't a lever; focus on insulin sensitivity tools (inositol, metformin if indicated) and letrozole.
- Over 35 with PCOS: skip prolonged trials of lifestyle alone; move to induction faster.
- GD risk: PCOS roughly doubles gestational diabetes risk, plan early pregnancy GD screening.
- Internal link: `lean-pcos-fertility`, `pcos-and-gd-link`.
- `<Cite n="2" />` 2023 PCOS Guideline pregnancy complications.

## How Long Does Each Step Take?
- Lifestyle + inositol: 3-6 months trial.
- Letrozole: typically 3-6 ovulatory cycles before escalating.
- IUI: 3-4 cycles before considering IVF.
- IVF: cycle-to-cycle, individualised.
- Honest framing: the typical full pathway is 6-18 months, not 6 weeks.

## NHS vs US Pathway (where dual-market relevant)
- Two-column `<Callout type="note">`:
  - UK NHS: GP referral, NHS fertility clinic for induction; letrozole still off-label in UK for ovulation induction but commonly prescribed by specialists. IVF access strictly NICE-criteria.
  - US: faster access via OB-GYN or REI; insurance coverage variable; letrozole prescribed routinely; IUI/IVF more readily available out-of-pocket.

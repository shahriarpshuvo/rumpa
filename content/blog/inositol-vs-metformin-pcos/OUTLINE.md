## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Open with the question I am asked weekly: "Should I be on inositol or metformin?" Reassure that inositol vs metformin for PCOS fertility is one of the most useful comparisons to understand because both target insulin resistance but differ on side effects, evidence base, and how fast they work.

## What Both Drugs Are Trying to Fix ← targets: "insulin resistance treatment PCOS"
- Insulin resistance drives PCOS in most (not all) phenotypes
- High insulin → high androgens → disrupted ovulation
- Improving insulin sensitivity restores ovulation in many patients
- Insulin resistance treatment in PCOS aims to break this loop
- `<Callout type="note">` Neither is a cure; both are tools

## How Inositol Works and How to Dose It ← targets: "myo-inositol dosing"
- Myo-inositol acts as a second messenger for insulin signalling
- D-chiro-inositol supports glucose disposal
- Standard myo-inositol dosing for fertility: 2 g twice daily (4 g/day)
- 40:1 myo-inositol to D-chiro-inositol ratio is the most studied formulation
- Onset: 8 to 12 weeks for menstrual regularity and ovulation effects
- Available over the counter (UK and US)
- `<Cite n="1" />` SOGC 2025 PCOS position statement

## How Metformin Works and What to Expect ← targets: "metformin side effects PCOS"
- Biguanide; reduces hepatic glucose output, improves peripheral insulin sensitivity
- Typical PCOS fertility dose: 500 mg titrated to 1500-2000 mg/day
- Off-label for PCOS-fertility use in the UK (per NICE), prescribed by GP/specialist
- Metformin side effects in PCOS: GI upset (nausea, diarrhoea) in up to 30%, often dose-dependent; B12 deficiency on long-term use; rare lactic acidosis
- Extended-release improves tolerability
- `<Cite n="2" />` NICE guidance on metformin in PCOS

## What the Evidence Shows on Fertility Outcomes ← targets: "inositol vs metformin PCOS fertility"
- Multiple RCTs show comparable improvements in HOMA-IR, SHBG, ovarian volume, and cycle regularity
- Some trials show inositol with higher ovulation and pregnancy rates over 3 months (e.g., 65% vs 50% ovulation; 30% vs 18% pregnancy in one cohort)
- A 2023 meta-analysis: 84% fewer side effects with myo-inositol vs metformin, similar metabolic gains
- Caveat: many inositol trials are smaller and shorter; metformin has the larger and longer evidence base across pregnancy outcomes and is the only one with formal NICE positioning
- `<Cite n="3" />` Sobral et al., Reproductive Female and Child Health 2025 meta-analysis
- `<Cite n="4" />` PubMed 2025 phenotype-stratified RCT

## Which Works Faster for PCOS Fertility ← targets: "which works faster for PCOS fertility"
- Both need around 3 months of consistent use before judging ovulation effect
- Inositol tends to be better tolerated, so adherence is often higher
- Metformin may have a slight edge in patients with overt impaired glucose tolerance or higher BMI
- ESHRE and SOGC guidelines support either as a reasonable first-line metabolic adjunct; metformin is the only option with regulatory positioning for ovulation induction support

## Can I Take Inositol and Metformin Together? ← targets: "can I take inositol and metformin together"
- Yes, combinations are studied; meta-analyses suggest combined therapy improves cycle regularity vs metformin alone
- May allow lower metformin doses, fewer GI side effects
- Always under clinician supervision; not a DIY stack
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Combination is reasonable in selected patients, not a default

## Choosing Between Them, What I Tell Patients ← bridges to Rumpa post / NHS-US pathway
- Lean PCOS, no IGT, mild symptoms: inositol first is reasonable
- Higher BMI, prediabetes, family history of type 2 diabetes: metformin first or combination
- Planning IVF: metformin co-treatment reduces OHSS risk, see [PCOS and IVF Success Rates Explained](/blog/ivf-with-pcos-success)
- Pair either with the lifestyle and tracking work in [PCOS and TTC, Tips That Help](/blog/pcos-ttc-tips)
- UK readers: GP can prescribe metformin off-label; US readers: any OB-GYN or REI

## What This Means for You (NOT "Conclusion")
- Inositol vs metformin for PCOS fertility is not a winner-takes-all choice; it is a phenotype-matched decision. Many patients do well on inositol, some need metformin, and a smaller group benefits from both. Discuss the route that fits your bloods, your tolerance, and your fertility timeline.

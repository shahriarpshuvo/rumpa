## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Deciding when to consider IVF after TTC is rarely a single moment: it is usually a slow build of months, tests, and conversations. Most patients ask me the same question, "How do I know when it is time for IVF?" The honest answer combines medical thresholds with personal readiness, and that is what this guide walks through.

## The Short Answer: Who Should Think About IVF Now ← targets: "when to consider IVF after TTC"
- Under 35: after 12 months of trying with normal workup
- 35-39: after 6 months
- 40+: evaluation and treatment often immediate
- Earlier if: blocked tubes, severe male factor, low AMH, recurrent loss, same-sex couple, single by choice
- `<Callout type="note">` These are starting points, not strict rules

## How NICE and ASRM Define the Threshold ← targets: "IVF decision criteria"
- NICE (UK): IVF after 2 years of unexplained infertility; 3 cycles under 40, 1 cycle 40-42
- ASRM (US): 12 months under 35, 6 months at 35+, immediate at 40+

## IUI Before IVF: Still the Default? ← targets: "IUI before IVF"
- NICE moved away from IUI as first-line in unexplained infertility; ESHRE 2023 reaffirms 3 cycles IUI-OS as reasonable first step
- When to skip IUI entirely (bilateral tubal block, severe male factor, age over 38 with low reserve)
- Cost and time trade-off

## Age and IVF Timing ← targets: "age and IVF timing"
- Egg quality decline curve, not a cliff
- AMH and antral follicle count as planning data, not predictions
- Why 38 is a meaningful inflection point for treatment escalation
- 40+ pathway: one NHS cycle if eligible, donor egg conversations

## The Medical Triggers That Skip the Waiting Period
- Bilateral tubal block on HSG
- Severely abnormal semen analysis (count, motility, morphology)
- Stage III/IV endometriosis
- Recurrent miscarriage workup positive
- Diminished ovarian reserve (low AMH for age, high FSH)
- Premature ovarian insufficiency
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Patients who come to me with these findings, I help them understand why waiting often costs more than it saves

## Questions to Ask Your Specialist Before Saying Yes
- What is my expected live birth rate per cycle by age?
- Is IUI a reasonable step first in my case?
- What is the protocol and why?
- What are cumulative success rates over 2-3 cycles?
- Costs, refunds, what happens if a cycle is cancelled

## What This Means for You
- Knowing when to consider IVF after TTC is less about waiting out a calendar and more about reading your own results, age, history, and life context together.

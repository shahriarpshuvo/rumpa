## The Two Main Recommendations (and Why They Differ) ← targets: "timed intercourse TTC"
- ACOG: every day or every other day during the fertile window (roughly cycle days 8 to 19 in a 26-32 day cycle).
- NHS: every 2 to 3 days across the whole cycle.
- Both are evidence-based. ACOG targets couples who can identify the fertile window. NHS targets couples who would rather not track and prefer the lower-pressure route.

## How the Fertile Window Actually Works ← targets: "fertile window sex frequency"
- Sperm survive in the female reproductive tract up to 5 days; the egg is viable for only 12 to 24 hours after ovulation.
- That gives a 6-day fertile window: 5 days before ovulation plus ovulation day itself.
- The highest per-cycle pregnancy chance comes from intercourse in the 2 to 3 days before ovulation, not on ovulation day.
- Soft bridge to `signs-of-ovulation-ttc` and `how-to-use-fertile-window-calculator`.

## Does Daily Sex Hurt Sperm Quality? ← targets: "sperm quality and frequency"
- Long-tail prose: "patients ask me 'is every other day better than every day for TTC?' as if daily intercourse depletes sperm."
- Current evidence: in men with normal semen parameters, daily ejaculation does not meaningfully reduce per-cycle pregnancy rates. In men with already low counts, every-other-day timing may be preferred.
- The "save it up" myth (long abstinence improves quality) is not supported; abstinence beyond 5 days can actually reduce sperm motility.

## What About the Other Question, Can You Have Too Much Sex? ← targets: long-tail "how much sex is too much when trying to conceive"
- Physiologically, no, daily sex during the fertile window will not reduce your chance.
- The real risk is emotional and relational. Scheduled intercourse is the most common reason couples burn out by month 4 of TTC.
- Practical middle path: identify a 6-day window using cycle tracking or LH testing, plan for intercourse every other day across that window, and treat the rest of the cycle as off-duty.

## When Frequency Stops Being the Issue ← bridges to the cornerstone and to escalation
- If you've timed correctly for 6 to 12 cycles and are not pregnant, frequency is no longer the lever to focus on.
- This is the point to consider basic fertility testing (ovulation confirmation, semen analysis, AMH, tubal patency).
- Bridge to `when-to-see-fertility-specialist` and `ttc-6-months-no-pregnancy`.
- UK vs US framing: NHS will assess after 12 months under 35 or 6 months at 35+; US private clinics will usually evaluate sooner.

## What Gestational Diabetes Actually Is (in 60 Seconds)
- Plain-English physiology: placental hormones blunt insulin, blood sugar rises after meals.
- Why diet is the first line: 70-85% of GD is controlled with diet + movement alone. `<Cite n="1" />` (ACOG Practice Bulletin / ADA 2026 Ch 15)
- Link out to spoke: [Did I cause my gestational diabetes?](/blog/did-i-cause-gestational-diabetes)

## Blood Sugar Targets in Pregnancy (NHS + ACOG/ADA)
- Targets secondary kw: "blood sugar targets pregnancy"
- NICE NG3 targets: fasting <5.3 mmol/L, 1-hr post-meal <7.8 mmol/L, 2-hr <6.4 mmol/L. `<Cite n="2" />`
- ADA 2026 / ACOG: fasting <95 mg/dL, 1-hr <140 mg/dL, 2-hr <120 mg/dL. `<Cite n="3" />`
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` A NICE-mmol vs ADA-mg/dL conversion table belongs here.

## The Plate Method, Your Simplest Starting Point
- Half plate non-starchy veg, quarter plate lean protein, quarter plate slow carbs, plus a small fat.
- Why the plate method beats calorie counting in early GD weeks.
- Bold takeaway: "If you remember nothing else, build every plate this way."

## Carbohydrate Counting in Pregnancy, the Next Level
- Targets secondary kw: "carbohydrate counting pregnancy"
- 30-45 g carbs per main meal, 15-30 g per snack as a typical start (individualised). `<Cite n="3" />`
- Breakfast is the hardest meal; insulin resistance peaks in the morning. Lower carbs at breakfast.
- How to read a label (carbs minus fibre for net), why fruit + dairy carbs count.

## Safe Foods for Gestational Diabetes (and What to Limit)
- Targets secondary kw: "safe foods for gestational diabetes"
- Green-light: non-starchy veg, eggs, paneer/tofu, fish, chicken, full-fat Greek yogurt, nuts, lentils, berries.
- Yellow-light (portion-controlled): basmati rice, roti, oats, sweet potato, fruit.
- Red-light: sugary drinks, juice, white bread, sugary cereals, sweets, sugary teas, "diet" pastries with hidden sugars.
- Long-tail in prose: "What foods to avoid with gestational diabetes?"
- `<Callout type="warning">` Honey, jaggery, dates: still carbs. Treat like sugar.

## A Sample Day Meal Plan for Gestational Diabetes (NHS + US Plate Version)
- Long-tail in prose: "Sample day meal plan for gestational diabetes"
- Breakfast: 2 eggs + 1 small slice wholegrain toast + spinach (NHS) / 2 eggs + ½ cup oats + berries (US).
- Mid-morning snack: Greek yogurt + walnuts.
- Lunch: grilled chicken salad + ¼ plate brown rice or 1 small roti.
- Afternoon snack: apple + peanut butter.
- Dinner: salmon + roasted veg + ½ cup quinoa or 1 roti.
- Bedtime snack: cottage cheese / handful nuts (prevents morning ketones, supports fasting). Link to spoke [Safe bedtime snacks for GD](/blog/gd-bedtime-snacks).

## South Asian Plate Swaps (Roti, Rice, Dal, Chai)
- Honest, practical: not "avoid rice", but "portion rice".
- Swap white rice → basmati + chana dal (lowers glycaemic load).
- Swap 2 chapatis → 1 chapati + bigger sabzi portion.
- Chai: switch to unsweetened or stevia; avoid sugary biscuits with it.
- Why this matters: South Asian women have 3-11x higher GD risk per BMI unit. `<Cite n="4" />`

## When Diet Isn't Enough, What Happens Next
- Link to spoke [Insulin vs Metformin for GD](/blog/gd-insulin-vs-metformin).
- Honest framing: needing insulin doesn't mean you failed diet, it means your placenta won. Common in second/third trimester.
- Continuous glucose monitor (CGM) is increasingly used; ADA 2026 expanded guidance. `<Cite n="3" />`

## Exercise as Part of Your Diet Plan
- 10-15 min walk after every meal cuts post-meal spikes dramatically.
- Resistance bands, prenatal yoga, swimming, all evidence-supported.
- Talk to your obstetric team before starting anything new.

## Common Mistakes Patients Make in the First Two Weeks
- Skipping breakfast (worsens fasting numbers).
- Cutting carbs too low (ketosis isn't the goal).
- Treating "sugar-free" as a free pass (sugar alcohols still spike some people).
- Not testing 1-hr post the first bite (not the last bite).

## What This Means for You
- Your gestational diabetes diet plan is a 6-12 week project, not a life sentence. Most patients I see in clinic regain insulin sensitivity within hours of delivery. Primary kw in last 200 words.

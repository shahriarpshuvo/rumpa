## Lifestyle changes that actually move semen parameters ← targets: "sperm quality lifestyle"
- Stop smoking and vaping, evidence on count and motility
- Alcohol under 14 units/week, ideally lower while TTC
- Weight: BMI above 30 lowers testosterone and raises DNA fragmentation
- Exercise 3-4 times/week, avoid extreme endurance training
- Sleep 7-8 hours, hormone pulse and repair pathways

## Heat, environment, and the underrated daily exposures
- Saunas, hot tubs, laptops on lap, tight underwear, dashboard heat in long commutes
- Endocrine disruptors: BPA, phthalates, pesticides (where evidence is solid vs hyped)
- Bicycle saddle and intense cycling, current state of the evidence

- "How long before TTC should men make changes?" answered in prose: at least 3 months, 6 if possible
- `<Callout type="note">` A normal semen analysis can still have high DNA fragmentation

## What This Means for You
- Male fertility pre-conception tips are not optional extras. Three months is the minimum useful window, and the simple list (stop smoking, drop alcohol, lose excess weight, sleep, keep the scrotum cool, add evidence-based supplements) covers most of the gain. If you suspect a male factor or have a history of loss, ask about semen analysis and DNA fragmentation early. `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`

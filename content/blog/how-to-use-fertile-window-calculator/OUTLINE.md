## What a Fertile Window Calculator Actually Does ← targets: "fertile window calculator"
- It takes your last menstrual period date and your average cycle length, then estimates ovulation around day (cycle length minus 14), and flags the 5-6 days before plus the day of as your fertile window.
- It is a calendar math tool, not a hormone reader.
- Long-tail answered in prose: "when is my fertile window".

## Step One: Get Your Cycle Length Right ← targets: "menstrual cycle length"
- How to count cycle length: day 1 (first day of full bleed) to the day before next period.
- Track 3 consecutive cycles; use the average.
- Cycle length variability under 7 days is normal; over 7 days is the threshold for "irregular".

## Step Two: Identify Your Most Fertile Days ← targets: "most fertile days"
- The 5 days before ovulation + the day of ovulation are the fertile window; the 2 days before ovulation are peak fertility.
- Sperm survive up to 5 days in fertile mucus; the egg lives ~12-24 hours after release.

## When a Fertile Window Calculator Fails You
- Irregular cycles (variability >7 days): calculator estimates become unreliable.
- PCOS: ovulation may not occur monthly or may shift dramatically; LH tests are also unreliable.
- Long-tail answered in prose: "are fertile window calculators accurate with PCOS".
- Post-pill cycles in the first 3 months, breastfeeding, perimenopause, stress, shift work.
- `<Callout type="warning">` If your calculator predictions and body signs disagree two cycles running, trust the body signs and book a review.
- Internal link: pcos-cycle-tracking-ovulation.

## NHS vs US Pathway When the Calculator Is Not Enough
- **UK / NHS:** GP can offer day-21 (mid-luteal) progesterone after a sensible TTC interval; pelvic scan and AMH typically need referral.
- **US:** OB-GYN can order the same workup, often without a waiting period.

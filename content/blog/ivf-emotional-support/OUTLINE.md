## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Open with the patient question: "Will I be able to cope?" Note that IVF emotional support, what to expect at each stage, is the single most common worry I hear in pre-cycle consultations. Reassure: the rollercoaster is normal, and structured help is available on day one.

## The Emotional Arc of an IVF Cycle ← targets: "IVF anxiety coping strategies"
- Down-regulation: low mood, hormone flatness
- Stimulation: bloating, mood swings, daily-injection fatigue
- Egg retrieval: relief + post-anaesthetic dip
- Embryo transfer: hope spike
- Two-week wait: peak anxiety window
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Up to 40% of patients report high anxiety in the 2WW. It is expected, not a sign anything is wrong.

## Coping in the Two-Week Wait ← targets: "two-week wait mental health"
- Short walks, gentle yoga, hobby blocks
- Limit fertility-forum scrolling (anxiety amplifier)
- Symptom-spotting trap: progesterone mimics pregnancy signs
- Plan one small "anchor" activity per day
- `<Cite n="1" />` HFEA guidance on emotional support

## Counselling and Professional Support ← targets: "IVF counselling UK and US"
- UK: every HFEA-licensed clinic must offer counselling by law; BICA directory; GP route to NHS talking therapies
- US: ASRM/SART mental health professional directory; clinic-affiliated psychologists; employer EAP programmes
- When to escalate: persistent low mood >2 weeks, intrusive thoughts, panic, sleep loss, relationship strain
- `<Cite n="2" />` SART emotional considerations

## Supporting a Partner Through IVF ← targets: "how to handle the emotional rollercoaster of IVF"
- Divide tasks, not feelings: injections vs admin vs appointments
- Agree a "no symptom debate" rule in the 2WW
- Schedule one non-IVF date per week
- Recognise that male partners often suppress feelings (HFEA blog on male infertility)

## What Support Is Available During IVF (UK and US Resources) ← bridges to Rumpa post / NHS-US pathway
- UK: Fertility Network UK, HFEA support pages, BICA counsellors
- US: RESOLVE, SART mental health directory
- Apps and peer groups (caveat: select moderated, evidence-based)
- Link to [How to Prepare for Your First IVF Cycle](/blog/first-ivf-cycle-prep) and [PCOS and IVF Success Rates Explained](/blog/ivf-with-pcos-success)

## What This Means for You (NOT "Conclusion")
- Stress does not cause IVF to fail, and feeling overwhelmed does not mean you are failing the cycle. IVF emotional support, what to expect and where to find it, is something you can map out before stims even start. Soft `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`.

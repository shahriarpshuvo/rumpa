## What "IVF Success Rate" Actually Measures ← targets: "IVF success rates by age"
- Three common denominators: per embryo transferred, per egg retrieval cycle, cumulative live birth rate across all transfers from one stimulation.
- Why "live birth" is the only number that matters for planning, not "clinical pregnancy".
- `<Cite n="1" />` HFEA reporting standards, `<Cite n="2" />` CDC ART definitions.

## UK Success Rates: HFEA Data ← targets: "HFEA UK IVF data"
- HFEA 2022 preliminary data: ~35% live birth per fresh embryo transferred for patients 18-34; ~10% per embryo at 40-42; ~5% per embryo at 43-44.
- Frozen embryo cycles have crept past fresh in many UK clinics.
- Average age of first-cycle UK patient is now over 35, which shifts the headline numbers when pooled.
- `<Callout type="note">` HFEA uses per-embryo-transferred as the standard headline.

## US Success Rates: CDC / SART Data ← targets: "CDC SART IVF data US"
- CDC/SART report cumulative live birth per intended egg retrieval, including all subsequent transfers within ~12 months.
- US cumulative live birth rate per retrieval is much higher than UK per-embryo headlines: under 35 cumulative live birth often exceeds 50%, dropping into single digits past 42.
- Long-tail answered in prose: "what is IVF success rate at 38" (approximately 25-35% per embryo transferred or cumulative ~40-45% per retrieval depending on source).
- `<Cite n="3" />` CDC ART success rates, `<Cite n="4" />` SART national summary.

## Why UK and US Numbers Look So Different
- HFEA per-embryo-transferred vs CDC SART per-retrieval cumulative are not the same denominator.
- Clinic case-mix differs: US patients average younger first-cycle, more elective single embryo transfer, freeze-all strategies.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` A 35% UK per-embryo number is not worse than a 50% US per-retrieval number. They count different things.

## NHS vs US Pathway for Funding Those Cycles
- **UK / NHS:** NHS-funded IVF varies by ICB, often 1 fresh cycle, age cap usually 40 or 42, BMI thresholds; private cycles £5-7k typical.
- **US:** Insurance varies wildly by state and employer; out-of-pocket cycles commonly $15-25k including medications.

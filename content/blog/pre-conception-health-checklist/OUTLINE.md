## How Far in Advance to Start Pre-Conception Care ← "how long before TTC to start pre-conception care"
- 3 months is the typical guidance: egg maturation cycle is ~90 days, sperm production cycle is ~74 days.
- Why folic acid alone isn't enough.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "In my clinic, women often ask if 3 months is enough. For most healthy patients yes. For those with PCOS, diabetes, thyroid disease or a previous loss, I usually want 6 months."
- `<Cite n="1" />` ACOG prepregnancy counseling; `<Cite n="2" />` CDC preconception care.

## Book a Pre-Conception Visit (NHS GP or US OB-GYN)
- What to ask for in the UK: GP appointment, BMI check, BP, smear up to date, rubella/varicella status, medication review.
- What to ask for in the US: OB-GYN or family-medicine preconception visit, insurance coverage rules.
- Both pathways: bring a medication list (including supplements and herbal).
- Internal link: `pre-pregnancy-bloods-uk`, `pre-pregnancy-bloods-us`.
- `<Cite n="3" />` ACOG Committee Opinion 762.

## Folic Acid and Other Pre-Conception Supplements ← "folic acid pre-conception"
- 400 mcg daily for most, started at least 1 month (ideally 3 months) before TTC.
- 5 mg high-dose: prior NTD pregnancy, BMI > 30, diabetes (Type 1/2), epilepsy meds, sickle-cell, coeliac, family history of NTD. PCOS not automatic but often discussed.
- Vitamin D 10 mcg (400 IU) daily in UK; 600 IU/day baseline in US.
- Iodine: 150 mcg/day (US ACOG); UK relies on diet but flag risk if no dairy/fish.
- Omega-3 DHA: optional supportive evidence.
- Internal link: `folic-acid-before-ttc` for the deep version.
- `<Cite n="4" />` NHS folic acid guidance; `<Cite n="5" />` ACOG nutrition during pregnancy.

## Pre-Pregnancy Blood Tests Worth Asking For ← "pre-pregnancy blood tests"
- Core panel: FBC, ferritin, TSH and free T4, HbA1c, vitamin D, rubella IgG, varicella IgG, hep B, HIV, syphilis.
- PCOS or irregular cycles: add fasting insulin, lipids, AMH if planning IVF timing question.
- South Asian readers: ferritin and vitamin D very often low; thalassaemia carrier screen (haemoglobinopathy) sensible if family origins suggest.
- Partner: where labs are appropriate (haemoglobinopathies, infectious disease screen).
- `<Callout type="note">` UK NHS coverage varies by GP; US insurance varies by plan.
- Internal link: `pre-pregnancy-bloods-uk`, `pre-pregnancy-bloods-us`.
- `<Cite n="6" />` NICE NG6 / RCOG guidance on screening.

## Weight, Activity, and Body Composition ← "weight and pre-conception fertility"
- Honest, non-shaming framing. BMI 18.5-25 is associated with the highest fertility, but recovery of cycles with even 5-10% weight change in PCOS is well-evidenced.
- 150 minutes/week moderate activity (NHS + ACOG aligned).
- Strength training 2x/week supports insulin sensitivity (relevant for PCOS readers).
- What not to do: crash diets, very low-calorie regimens, aggressive cardio that disrupts cycles.
- Internal link: `weight-and-pre-conception-fertility`.
- `<Cite n="7" />` NICE CG156 fertility guideline.

## Optimising Chronic Conditions Before You Conceive
- Diabetes: target HbA1c < 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) before TTC; safer medications switch (often metformin, off ACE inhibitors, off statins).
- Thyroid: aim TSH ideally < 2.5 mIU/L pre-conception per most guidelines.
- Hypertension: switch ACE/ARB to labetalol or nifedipine before TTC.
- Mental health: SSRI safety, never stop abruptly without psychiatric review.
- Epilepsy: review of teratogenic risk meds (especially sodium valproate).
- `<Callout type="warning">` Never stop a prescribed medication on your own. Plan the switch with your GP or specialist 3 months ahead.
- `<Cite n="3" />` ACOG prepregnancy counseling.

## Vaccines, Infections, and Dental Care
- Confirm rubella, varicella, MMR, Tdap (US), influenza, hep B.
- Wait 1 month after MMR/varicella before TTC.
- COVID-19 vaccine: discuss current schedule with GP/OB.
- Dental cleaning: gum disease linked to adverse pregnancy outcomes.
- Cervical screening up to date.
- `<Cite n="8" />` CDC preconception immunisation; `<Cite n="9" />` ACOG immunisation in pregnancy.

## Lifestyle: Alcohol, Smoking, Caffeine, Cannabis ← South Asian context note
- Alcohol: no safe amount once TTC. Pre-TTC, reduce to none.
- Smoking and vaping: cessation by both partners; vaping is not "safe" for fertility.
- Caffeine: 200 mg/day cap.
- Recreational drugs and cannabis: stop before TTC; cannabis affects sperm motility and ovulation regularity.
- Cultural notes: prevalence of tobacco chewing (paan, gutka) in South Asian diaspora; address directly without judgement.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "Patients who come to me with this question, I help them set a date 3 months out and treat it as a project, not a punishment."

## Pre-Conception Health for Your Partner ← "partner pre-conception health"
- Sperm regenerates every ~74 days, so 3 months matters here too.
- Reduce alcohol, stop smoking, manage weight.
- Boxer vs brief doesn't matter much; heat exposure (hot tubs, laptop on lap, sauna) does.
- Folic acid for him? Modest evidence for 400 mcg + zinc.
- When to ask for a semen analysis (link to dedicated spoke).
- Internal link: `male-fertility-pre-conception`, `sperm-analysis-partner-test`.
- `<Cite n="10" />` ESHRE / NICE CG156 male fertility.

## Stopping Birth Control and Cycle Tracking
- How long fertility takes to return after each method (link out for the full FAQ).
- Track 2-3 cycles to learn your fertile window before stressing the timing.
- When to seek help: under 35 after 12 months, over 35 after 6 months, immediately if known PCOS/endometriosis/prior surgery.

## NHS vs US Pathway
- Two-column `<Callout type="note">` summarising:
  - UK NHS: free GP preconception chat, free folic acid/vitamin D advice, NHS bloods variable by area, smear via NHS recall, vaccines free.
  - US: insurance-dependent preconception visit, ACA preventive care includes some screens, prenatal vitamins OTC, dental coverage often separate.

## A Realistic 3-Month Pre-Conception Timeline
- Month 1: GP/OB visit booked, start folic acid + vit D, medication review, baseline bloods.
- Month 2: vaccine catch-up, dental, lifestyle changes embed, partner workup if indicated.
- Month 3: track cycles, finalise med switches, mental health check-in, stop contraception per plan.

## Set Expectations: What "Normal" Looks Like ← targets: "trying to conceive tips"
- About 80% of couples conceive within 12 months; roughly half within 6 months
- Age effect: under 30 cycles vs over 35
- Why month 3-6 is when the anxiety spike usually happens
- Long-tail in prose: "what to do in first 6 months of TTC"

## Map Your Fertile Window ← targets: "fertile window"
- 6-day window: 5 days before ovulation + ovulation day
- Cervical mucus changes as the most reliable cheap signal
- LH urine tests (OPKs) as confirmation
- BBT as a backwards-looking confirmation, not a predictor
- Link to how-to-use-fertile-window-calculator and bbt-accuracy-ttc spokes
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` In clinic I tell patients: do not chase a single ovulation day, treat it as a window

## How Often to Have Sex (Without the Pressure) ← targets: "trying to conceive tips"
- NHS: every 2-3 days throughout cycle
- ACOG: every 1-2 days in fertile window
- Why "performance" timing often backfires
- Link to how-often-have-sex-to-conceive spoke
- `<Callout type="note">` Both approaches work; pick the one that fits your relationship

## Pre-Conception Health Foundations ← targets: "pre-conception health"
- Folic acid (400 mcg in the US; 400 mcg NHS, higher if BMI > 30 or epilepsy meds)
- Stop smoking, limit alcohol, caffeine moderation
- Cervical screening, rubella status, vaccinations
- Both partners: lifestyle matters for sperm too
- South Asian context: vitamin D and B12 status often warrants screening
- `<Cite n="2" />` ACOG Prepregnancy Counseling committee opinion
- Link to pre-conception-health-checklist (sibling cornerstone)

## Month-by-Month: A Calm 6-Month Plan
- **Month 1-2:** Start folic acid, stop birth control if still on it, learn your cycle length, baseline GP/PCP visit
- **Month 3-4:** Begin fertile window tracking, partner lifestyle audit, optimise sleep and stress
- **Month 5-6:** Re-evaluate: are cycles regular, are you hitting the window, are there any red-flag symptoms

## Red Flags Worth Calling Your Doctor About ← targets: "TTC first 6 months"
- Cycles shorter than 21 or longer than 35 days
- No bleed for 3+ months
- Heavy/painful periods (possible endometriosis)
- History of pelvic infection, surgery, or chemotherapy
- Known male-factor history
- Age over 35

## The Mental Load of TTC ← targets: "TTC first 6 months"
- The "two-week wait" reality
- Social media and "is it me" spirals
- When to step back from tracking
- Talking to your partner about workload sharing (calendar tracking, OPKs, appointments)
- `<Callout type="warning">` Anxiety and ovulation interact; sustainable habits beat perfect tracking

## Common Myths to Drop in Month One
- "You need to lie still for 30 minutes after sex" (no evidence)
- "Cough syrup thins cervical mucus" (anecdotal, not evidence-based)
- "Pineapple core boosts implantation" (no evidence)
- "Stress causes infertility" (oversimplified, evidence is mixed)

## NHS vs US Pathway
- Two-column callout:
  - **UK / NHS:** Free GP visit, NHS pre-conception advice, free folic acid via prescription if eligible, GP-led fertility workup at 12 months (6 if 35+)
  - **US:** Insurance-driven primary care visit, OB-GYN often does initial workup, AMH may be self-pay, escalation at 12 months under 35 / 6 months at 35+

## What This Means for You
- Use these trying to conceive tips as a 6-month framework, not a stress checklist. Most couples conceive in this window; for those who do not, you will be far better placed to have an informed conversation with your doctor. If something on the red-flag list resonates, do not wait the full 6 months. `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`

## Why IVF Cost in the UK Is So Confusing (Advertised vs True Price)
- HFEA-set context: clinics set their own prices, no standard package.
- Advertised package vs the final invoice (medication, blood tests, viral screening, embryo storage often excluded).
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Ask every clinic for an "all-in" quote before signing.

## NHS IVF Eligibility, What NICE Recommends vs What ICBs Actually Fund
- Targets secondary kw: "NHS IVF eligibility"
- NICE CG156 still recommends up to 3 full cycles for women under 40, 1 cycle for 40-42 meeting criteria. `<Cite n="1" />`
- Reality: 72% of ICBs fund 1 cycle, ~10% fund 3. Postcode lottery.
- Common ICB criteria: age 23-39 (some up to 42), BMI 19-30 (some 19-35), no living children, 1-2 years TTC without success, non-smoker.
- Long-tail in prose: "Does the NHS pay for IVF in all areas?" Short answer, no.

## How Much Does One IVF Cycle Cost in the UK at a Private Clinic?
- Targets secondary kw: "private IVF clinic London cost"
- Advertised: £3,500-£5,000 base.
- Realistic all-in: £5,000-£8,000 per cycle (London skews higher).
- Regional spread: London > Manchester/Birmingham > Northern Ireland/Scotland.
- Long-tail in prose: "How much does one IVF cycle cost in the UK?"

## Hidden IVF Fees UK Patients Get Caught By
- Targets secondary kw: "hidden IVF fees UK"
- Medication £1,500-£2,500 (stims, trigger, progesterone).
- HFEA fee (~£80-£90 per cycle).
- Viral screening, AMH, semen analysis.
- ICSI uplift £1,000-£1,500 if needed.
- Embryo freezing £350-£500/year storage.
- Add-ons like time-lapse imaging, EmbryoGlue, endometrial scratch, PGT-A (HFEA "add-ons" traffic light system, most are amber or red on evidence). `<Cite n="2" />`
- `<Callout type="warning">` HFEA's add-ons review is the honest reference, not the clinic brochure.

## NHS vs Private Pathway, A Side-by-Side
- Two-column callout / pathway notes:
  - **NHS pathway:** GP referral, fertility clinic workup, ICB funding decision, waitlist (often 6-18 months), 1-3 funded cycles depending on postcode, prescription charges still apply.
  - **Private pathway:** Self-refer, initial consult (£200-£350), workup, start within weeks, pay per cycle, no waitlist.
- When private genuinely makes sense: age 40+, AMH dropping, already declined by ICB, second cycle after NHS failure.

## How Soon Can I Get Pregnant After Stopping the Pill? ← "how soon can I get pregnant after stopping the pill"
- Combined pill: ovulation usually returns within 1-3 cycles, sometimes the first.
- Progesterone-only pill (POP/mini-pill): can return within days.
- Most women are at the same baseline fertility as never-used-pill women by 3 months.
- The pill does not "stay in your system" or "deplete your eggs". Decades of evidence.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "In my clinic, I see women blame the pill for slow conception when the real factor is usually age or undiagnosed PCOS that the pill was masking."
- `<Cite n="1" />` NHS contraception and periods; `<Cite n="2" />` Tommy's stopping contraception.

## Fertility Return After IUD (Hormonal and Copper) ← "IUD removal fertility return"
- Copper IUD: fertility returns immediately, often within the first cycle.
- Hormonal IUD (Mirena, Kyleena): cycle resumes within 1-3 months; one study showed average time to pregnancy of 4.4 months and 97% pregnant within 18 months in young healthy women.
- You can TTC the same cycle the IUD is removed.
- `<Cite n="3" />` PubMed IUD removal fertility studies.

## Fertility Return After the Implant and Depo-Provera ← "implant Depo fertility return time" / "how long does Depo take to leave your system"
- Implant (Nexplanon): hormone clears within a week, ovulation usually within 3-4 weeks.
- Depo-Provera (DMPA injection): the slowest. Average return to ovulation is 5-7 months after the last shot, and can take up to 12-18 months for some women.
- This is delayed clearance, not infertility. It does not damage fertility long-term.
- `<Callout type="note">` "Patients who come to me wanting to conceive within 6 months, I usually suggest switching off Depo at least 9-12 months before TTC."
- `<Cite n="4" />` FSRH UK guidance on DMPA.

## When Periods Don't Return: Post-Pill Amenorrhea ← "post-pill amenorrhea"
- Most women have a normal cycle within 3 months.
- If no period after 3 months off the pill, it is worth investigating. Common findings:
  - Undiagnosed PCOS (the pill was hiding irregular cycles).
  - Low body weight or recent significant weight loss.
  - Thyroid dysfunction.
  - Hypothalamic amenorrhea from over-exercise.
- This is not a pill side-effect, it is something the pill was concealing.
- Internal link: `how-to-know-if-you-have-pcos`.
- `<Cite n="5" />` NICE CG156 anovulation work-up.

## What to Do in the First 3 Months After Stopping
- Start folic acid 400 mcg (5 mg if you have qualifying conditions).
- Track 1-2 cycles to learn your fertile window.
- Don't expect period regularity in cycle 1, especially after long-term pill use.
- See your GP for pre-conception bloods.
- Internal link: `folic-acid-before-ttc`, `pre-conception-health-checklist`.

## When to See Your GP or Doctor
- No period 3+ months after stopping any method.
- Under 35 and trying for 12 months without success.
- Over 35 and trying for 6 months without success.
- Any cycle that returns very heavy, very painful, or unusually irregular.
- Internal link: `when-to-see-fertility-specialist`.

- Two-column `<Callout type="note">`:

  - US: insurance-dependent removal, fertility work-up at 12 months under 35 or 6 months over 35 (ASRM definition).

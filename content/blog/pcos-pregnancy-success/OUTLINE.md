## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- When a patient with PCOS sits down for the first time, the question underneath every other question is "will I actually have a baby". The honest answer, from the evidence, is yes for most women, and PCOS pregnancy success stories are far more common than the panic on Google suggests. Here is what the data shows on natural conception, ovulation induction, and IVF, with the numbers your clinic might not quote.

## How often do women with PCOS get pregnant? ← targets: "how often do women with PCOS get pregnant"
- A longitudinal cohort showed women with PCOS had similar lifetime fertility outcomes to women without PCOS, just often later and with more support `<Cite n="1" />`.
- One-year natural conception in the same cohort, around 37% in women with PCOS attempting pregnancy `<Cite n="2" />`.
- Why "rates" depend so heavily on age, BMI and phenotype.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "PCOS is one of the most treatable causes of infertility."

## Natural conception with PCOS, what the numbers look like ← targets: "natural conception with PCOS"
- Lifestyle, weight where relevant, cycle tracking, vitamin D and inositol.
- Time-to-pregnancy is longer on average, but cumulative live-birth is similar over 5-10 years.
- Link to [PCOS and TTC, Tips That Help](/blog/pcos-ttc-tips).

## PCOS pregnancy rates with ovulation induction ← targets: "PCOS pregnancy rates"
- Letrozole live-birth rate 27.5%, clomiphene 19.1% per cycle in PPCOS II `<Cite n="3" />`.
- 6-cycle cumulative live-birth on letrozole, around 50-60% in trial populations.
- When ovulation induction is enough vs when to escalate.
- Link to [Letrozole vs Clomid for PCOS Ovulation](/blog/letrozole-vs-clomid-pcos).

## PCOS IVF outcomes, similar live birth but higher OHSS risk ← targets: "PCOS IVF outcomes"
- Meta-analyses show similar clinical pregnancy and live birth per started cycle compared with women without PCOS `<Cite n="4" />`.
- OHSS risk roughly 3x higher (around 7.5% vs 2.7%), which is why protocols are adjusted.
- Early pregnancy loss after IVF higher in PCOS, more so with high BMI or untreated insulin resistance.
- Link to [PCOS and IVF Success Rates Explained](/blog/ivf-with-pcos-success).

## What helped real women conceive with PCOS ← targets: "what helped real women conceive with PCOS"
- Composite, no identifying details: the patterns I see in clinic, regular cycle tracking, treating insulin resistance, sleep, and not delaying specialist input past 6-9 months.
- `<Callout type="note">` Patients who come to me after a year of trying, I help them understand that PCOS is rarely a no, it is usually a route.

## Risks worth knowing in pregnancy ← bridges to related Rumpa post
- Higher rates of gestational diabetes, hypertension and preterm birth in PCOS pregnancies `<Cite n="5" />`.
- This is monitored, not avoided.
- Link to [PCOS and Gestational Diabetes, the Link](/blog/pcos-and-gd-link).

## What This Means for You
- The honest read on PCOS pregnancy success stories is that the data is reassuring, most women with PCOS will conceive and carry, often with a little help. The plan matters more than the panic.

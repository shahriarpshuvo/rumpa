## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Open with the patient question: "What should I do 90 days before IVF?" Note that how to prepare for your first IVF cycle is the question I get from almost every patient in their first pre-IVF appointment. Frame: the 90 days before stims are the only window where lifestyle changes can meaningfully reach the egg cohort being recruited.

## The 90-Day Window, Why It Matters ← targets: "what should I do 90 days before IVF"
- Folliculogenesis takes about 3 months from antral recruitment to retrieval
- Anything you change in the last 90 days reaches the cohort being matured
- Set up: diet, sleep, alcohol, smoking, exercise baseline
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Pick 2-3 changes you can keep, not 10 you cannot

## Pre-Cycle Bloods and Scans, What to Expect ← targets: "IVF pre-cycle bloods and scans"
- AMH, FSH, LH, oestradiol, TSH, prolactin, vitamin D
- Antral follicle count (AFC) via transvaginal scan
- Saline infusion sonogram or hysteroscopy if uterine concerns
- Partner: semen analysis, infection screen
- UK: HFEA-licensed clinic workup; US: clinic-specific protocol
- `<Cite n="1" />` HFEA / NICE on baseline assessment

## Supplements Before IVF, What the Evidence Supports ← targets: "what supplements to take before IVF"
- Prenatal with 400 to 800 mcg folic acid: standard
- Vitamin D, replete to 75 nmol/L+ if deficient
- Omega-3 (DHA/EPA): supportive evidence for embryo quality
- CoQ10 200 to 600 mg/day: mitochondrial support, started 8-12 weeks before stims, evidence strongest in women >35 or with prior poor response
- DHEA and melatonin: case-selected, doctor-prescribed only, not blanket recommendations
- Avoid: high-dose antioxidant cocktails marketed online with no trial data
- `<Cite n="2" />` Nutritional supplements and IVF, PubMed evidence-based review

## Diet, Exercise and Sleep ← targets: "IVF stimulation phase"
- Mediterranean-pattern diet, lean protein, leafy greens, whole grains, oily fish
- Caffeine: 1 cup/day cap
- Alcohol: minimise pre-stims, stop before egg collection
- Exercise during the IVF stimulation phase: low impact only (yoga, Pilates, walking); avoid running and HIIT due to ovarian-torsion risk as ovaries enlarge
- Sleep 7 to 9 hours; melatonin secretion supports oocyte quality
- `<Callout type="warning">` Stop high-impact exercise the moment stims begin

## Egg Retrieval Recovery, What the Next 7 Days Look Like ← targets: "egg retrieval recovery"
- Day-of-retrieval: short sedation, vaginal-probe needle aspiration
- Expect bloating, mild cramping, light spotting for 24-72 hours
- Watch for OHSS red flags: rapid weight gain >2 kg/day, reduced urine output, severe abdominal pain, breathlessness
- Pelvic rest, hydration with electrolytes, no heavy lifting for ~7 days
- `<Cite n="3" />` HFEA / SART post-retrieval guidance

## Mental, Logistical and Work Prep ← bridges to Rumpa post
- Block out time for monitoring appointments (every 2-3 days during stims)
- Tell one trusted person at work if helpful
- Plan childcare for retrieval day
- Read [IVF Emotional Support, What to Expect](/blog/ivf-emotional-support) before stims start
- UK readers: book counselling slot at your clinic from the start

## What This Means for You (NOT "Conclusion")
- How to prepare for your first IVF cycle is less about doing everything perfectly and more about doing a small set of evidence-backed things consistently across 90 days. Plan the bloods, the supplements your clinician agrees with, the diet, the gentle exercise, and the support, and you go into stims with the best version of the cycle.

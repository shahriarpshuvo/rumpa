## How Long Before TTC You Should Start ← "how many months before TTC should I take folic acid"
- Minimum: 1 month before TTC per NHS and CDC.
- Optimal: 3 months before TTC. Reason: full erythrocyte folate saturation takes ~12 weeks.
- The neural tube closes at 28 days post-conception, before many women know they are pregnant.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "In my clinic, I usually advise women to start the same day they decide to stop contraception, even if the actual TTC plan is months out."

## The Right Dose: 400 mcg vs 5 mg ← "folic acid dose pre-conception" / "high-dose folic acid 5 mg"
- 400 micrograms (mcg) per day: the standard for most women.
- 5 milligrams (mg, ~12x stronger) per day: prescribed if any of:
  - BMI > 30
  - Diabetes (Type 1 or Type 2)
  - Previous pregnancy affected by a neural tube defect
  - Family history of NTD in either partner
  - Taking anti-epileptic medication
  - Sickle cell disease, thalassaemia, or coeliac disease
- 5 mg is prescription-only in the UK.
- `<Cite n="1" />` NHS; `<Cite n="3" />` Tommy's.

## Do I Need 5 mg with PCOS or Diabetes? ← "do I need 5 mg folic acid with PCOS or diabetes"
- Diabetes (Type 1/2): yes, 5 mg from at least 3 months pre-conception is current UK guidance.
- PCOS without diabetes or high BMI: no specific 5 mg requirement, 400 mcg is fine.
- PCOS + BMI > 30: yes, 5 mg under your GP.
- Gestational diabetes in a previous pregnancy is not, by itself, a 5 mg indication, but discuss it with your GP.
- `<Callout type="note">` "Patients who come to me with this question, I help them check three boxes (BMI, diabetes status, prior NTD) and we decide with their GP."

## Why Folic Acid Matters: Neural Tube Defect Prevention ← "neural tube defect prevention"
- NTDs include spina bifida and anencephaly.
- Folate supplementation cuts NTD risk by 50-70%.
- The brain and spine form in weeks 3-4 of pregnancy.
- Folic acid is also tied to reduced risk of cleft lip/palate and some congenital heart defects.
- UK does not fortify flour with folate (consultation ongoing); US has fortified since 1998. This is why UK women may start with lower baseline folate stores.
- `<Cite n="4" />` ACOG nutrition in pregnancy; `<Cite n="5" />` PubMed review of folate and NTD.

## Methylfolate, Food Folate, and Prenatal Vitamins
- Prenatal vitamins usually contain 400-800 mcg of folic acid; check the label.
- L-methylfolate / 5-MTHF marketed for women with MTHFR variants; for most healthy women, standard folic acid works just as well.
- Food sources (leafy greens, lentils, fortified cereals) help but rarely hit 400 mcg reliably, so a supplement remains the recommendation.
- Stopping after 12 weeks is fine for NTD prevention, but many women continue throughout pregnancy in a combined prenatal.

## NHS vs US Pathway (where dual-market relevant)
- Two-column `<Callout type="note">`:
  - UK: 400 mcg OTC, 5 mg prescription, NHS GP can advise free of charge.
  - US: 400-800 mcg in any standard prenatal, 4 mg/5 mg by prescription, ACA covers prenatal counselling.

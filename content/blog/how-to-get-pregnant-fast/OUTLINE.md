## What "Fast" Realistically Means by Age ← "what helps you get pregnant faster"
- Per-cycle probability at peak fertility: ~25-30% under 30, ~20% at 30-34, ~15% at 35-37, ~10% at 38-40.
- 84% of under-35 couples conceive within 12 months of regular TTC.
- "Fast" usually means within 3-6 cycles. Even with perfect technique, biology has a ceiling.

## Nail the Fertile Window ← "optimise fertility window"
- The 6-day window: 5 days before ovulation plus the day of ovulation.
- Peak conception probability: the day before and the day of ovulation (~30% per encounter at peak).
- Sperm survives 3-5 days in fertile cervical mucus; eggs survive ~12-24 hours.
- How to identify ovulation: LH tests (OPKs), cervical mucus changes, basal body temperature (confirms after the fact, not predictive), cycle apps with caution.
- Internal link: `signs-of-ovulation-ttc`, `how-to-use-fertile-window-calculator`, `bbt-accuracy-ttc`.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "In my clinic, I see women obsess over the exact ovulation day. The honest advice: sex every other day across days 8 to 20 of a 28-day cycle covers nearly everyone."
- `<Cite n="3" />` NEJM Wilcox et al. 1995 timing of intercourse.

## How Often to Have Sex to Conceive ← "how to increase chance of pregnancy each cycle"
- NHS: every 2-3 days throughout the cycle is simpler and works for most couples.
- ACOG: every day or every other day during the fertile window.
- Daily sex does not lower sperm quality in fertile men, but every-other-day reduces TTC stress for most couples.
- Position, post-sex leg-up, or sex at a particular time of day: no robust evidence any of this matters.
- Internal link: `how-often-have-sex-to-conceive`.

## Pre-Conception Health That Actually Moves the Needle ← "pre-conception health"
- Folic acid 400 mcg daily from 3 months pre-TTC (5 mg if qualifying).
- BMI in the 19-30 range improves cycle regularity and ovulation in PCOS specifically.
- Stop smoking and vaping (both partners), limit alcohol, cap caffeine at 200 mg.
- Sleep 7-9 hours; chronic disruption affects LH pulsatility.
- Manage chronic conditions (diabetes HbA1c < 48 mmol/mol, thyroid TSH ideally < 2.5 mIU/L).
- Internal link: `pre-conception-health-checklist`, `weight-and-pre-conception-fertility`.
- `<Cite n="5" />` ACOG prepregnancy counseling.

## Male Partner Factors (Skip This and You Skip Half the Equation) ← "conceive quickly"
- Sperm regenerates ~74 days, so changes today affect quality in 3 months.
- Heat exposure (hot tubs, saunas, laptops on lap), tobacco, cannabis, anabolic steroids: all reduce sperm quality.
- Semen analysis is cheap, fast, and reveals 30-40% of fertility issues, yet routinely skipped.
- Internal link: `male-fertility-pre-conception`, `sperm-analysis-partner-test`.
- `<Cite n="6" />` ESHRE male factor.

## What Doesn't Help (and What to Stop Wasting Money On)
- Expensive "fertility tea" blends: no evidence.
- Seed cycling: no evidence.
- Lying still after sex with legs in the air: no evidence.
- Vitex / chaste tree: weak evidence, can disrupt cycles.
- Detoxes, womb cleanses, lymphatic massages for fertility: no evidence.
- Coq10, myo-inositol, vitamin D: evidence exists for specific groups (egg quality, PCOS, deficiency); not blanket "boosters".
- `<Callout type="warning">` Don't replace a GP visit with supplements.

## Tracking Tools: OPKs, BBT, and Cycle Apps
- OPKs (urine LH tests): identify the 24-36 hour fertile peak. Most useful single tool.
- BBT charting: confirms ovulation in retrospect, useful for pattern but not for timing.
- Cycle apps: helpful for non-PCOS, regular cycles; misleading for irregular cycles.
- Smart-bracelet temperature trackers: improving but not validated for PCOS.
- Internal link: `pcos-cycle-tracking-ovulation` for PCOS-specific tracking.

## Special Cases: PCOS, Irregular Cycles, and Endometriosis
- PCOS: cycle irregularity is the main blocker; ovulation induction (letrozole) is often the fast track to conception.
- Endometriosis: timing alone won't overcome anatomical/inflammatory factors; specialist referral often required.
- After 35 or with known risk factors: skip the "6 months waiting" rule and seek workup earlier.
- Internal link: `pcos-fertility-treatment-options`, `pcos-ttc-tips`.
- `<Cite n="7" />` 2023 International PCOS Guideline.

## When "Trying for Fast" Becomes "Seek Help"
- Under 35: investigate after 12 months.
- 35-40: investigate after 6 months.
- Over 40: investigate at the start.
- Irregular cycles, known PCOS or endometriosis, previous pelvic surgery, previous chemotherapy, two or more miscarriages: investigate immediately.
- Internal link: `ttc-6-months-no-pregnancy`, `when-to-see-fertility-specialist`, `unexplained-infertility-next-steps`.

## Is 6 Months Long Enough to Worry? ← targets: "when to seek fertility help"
- Key points to cover:
  - Statistic: roughly 80% of couples under 35 conceive within 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse, per NICE.
  - Standard guidance: under 35 wait until 12 months, 35+ seek help at 6 months.
  - Answer the long-tail in prose: "Is 6 months of TTC long enough to see a doctor?"
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` distinguishing "worry" from "investigate".

## Red Flags That Justify an Earlier Visit ← targets: "initial fertility investigations"
- Key points:
  - Irregular or absent periods, suspected PCOS/PMOS.
  - Severe period pain, suspected endometriosis.
  - Prior pelvic surgery, ectopic, or PID.
  - Known thyroid disorder, prior chemo, prior miscarriages.
  - Partner factors (prior testicular surgery, mumps, varicocele).
  - Clinical disclaimer: this list is not exhaustive; speak to a GP if anything feels off.

## What Your GP or OB-GYN Will Check First ← targets: "initial fertility investigations"
- Key points:
  - Cycle history and ovulation confirmation (day 21 progesterone in NHS; mid-luteal progesterone in US).
  - Hormone panel: TSH, prolactin, FSH/LH, AMH where indicated.
  - Pelvic ultrasound for antral follicle count and uterine anatomy.
  - Note: NHS pathway often staged; US private labs run a fuller panel earlier.
  - `<Cite n="1" />` for NICE NG257 referral threshold.

## Don't Forget Your Partner ← targets: "partner sperm test"
- Key points:
  - Male factor contributes to roughly half of all fertility issues.
  - A semen analysis is cheap, quick, non-invasive, and changes the workup dramatically.
  - Why couples skip it (cultural/discomfort) and why they shouldn't.

## NHS vs US Pathway, What to Expect Next ← targets: long-tail bridge
- Key points:
  - UK: GP refers to NHS fertility services after the 1-year mark (or earlier if 36+ or red flags). Postcode lottery for IVF funding.
  - US: Direct OB-GYN or reproductive endocrinologist referral; insurance varies dramatically by state.

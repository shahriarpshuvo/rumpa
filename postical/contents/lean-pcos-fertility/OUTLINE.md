## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- If your BMI is in the normal range but your periods are months apart, your acne will not settle and your GP has told you that you cannot have PCOS, you might be looking at lean PCOS symptoms fertility doctors still under-diagnose. Around one in five women with PCOS sit at a normal weight, and the fertility picture is not lighter, just quieter. Here is what I check for in clinic, and what it means if you are trying for a baby.

## What lean PCOS actually is ← targets: "lean PCOS diagnosis"
- Rotterdam criteria (2 of 3): irregular ovulation, clinical or biochemical hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovarian morphology on scan `<Cite n="1" />`.
- "Lean" means BMI under 25, not "mild PCOS".
- Why so many women hear "you cannot have PCOS, you are not overweight" and what that costs them.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "Lean PCOS is a diagnosis of pattern, not weight."

## The symptom picture in normal BMI PCOS ← targets: "normal BMI PCOS"
- Long or absent cycles (often 35-60+ days).
- Androgen signals: persistent acne after the teens, hirsutism on chin or chest, hair thinning at the crown.
- Mood and energy changes that get mislabelled as stress.
- Often normal lipids and normal weight, which is exactly why it gets missed.
- `<Callout type="note">` Patients who come to me with this question, I ask about cycle length over the last 12 months, not last 3.

## Insulin resistance in lean PCOS, the missed driver ← targets: "insulin resistance in lean PCOS"
- Up to 75% of lean PCOS women still show insulin resistance on a glucose tolerance test or HOMA-IR, even with a flat tummy `<Cite n="2" />`.
- Why a fasting glucose alone misses it.
- Inositol, metformin and lifestyle, what the evidence supports in lean phenotypes.
- Link to [Inositol vs Metformin for PCOS Fertility](/blog/inositol-vs-metformin-pcos).

## Does lean PCOS affect fertility differently? ← targets: "does lean PCOS affect fertility differently"
- The good news: better response to ovulation induction on average, lower OHSS risk in IVF compared with classic PCOS `<Cite n="3" />`.
- The hard part: cycles are so irregular that timed intercourse alone often fails, and patients spend months "trying naturally" with no ovulation.
- Egg quality is usually preserved, AMH often high.

## Can you have PCOS without being overweight? ← targets: "can you have PCOS without being overweight"
- Yes, and around 20% of PCOS phenotypes are lean `<Cite n="4" />`.
- The workup I run: day 2-5 bloods (LH, FSH, testosterone, SHBG, free androgen index, prolactin, TSH, AMH), pelvic ultrasound, HbA1c or 75g OGTT.
- When the answer is not PCOS, what else it could be (thyroid, hyperprolactinemia, hypothalamic amenorrhoea).

## Your fertility plan if you have lean PCOS ← bridges to related Rumpa post
- Track ovulation properly (OPK plus mid-luteal progesterone), not just an app.
- Talk to a fertility specialist sooner if cycles are over 35 days or absent.
- Link to [PCOS and TTC, Tips That Help](/blog/pcos-ttc-tips) and [Letrozole vs Clomid for PCOS Ovulation](/blog/letrozole-vs-clomid-pcos).

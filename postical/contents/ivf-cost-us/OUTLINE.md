## What One IVF Cycle Actually Costs in the US (Base + Medication + Add-Ons)
- Targets long-tail: "average out-of-pocket IVF cost US"
- Average cycle: $15,000-$25,000 self-pay. Median around $23k all-in. `<Cite n="1" />`
- Base IVF: $9,000-$14,000. Medication: $3,000-$7,000. ICSI: $1,500-$2,500. PGT-A: $4,800-$6,000. Frozen embryo transfer (FET): $4,000-$6,000.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Always ask if the quote includes anesthesia, embryo freezing year 1, and monitoring ultrasounds.

## Does Insurance Cover IVF in the US? The 4 Payer Realities
- Targets long-tail: "does insurance cover IVF in the US"
- Only ~25% of insured Americans have IVF coverage. `<Cite n="2" />`
- Four realities:
  1. State-mandate state with full IVF coverage.
  2. Employer-sponsored fertility benefit (Progyny, Carrot, Maven, WIN).
  3. Partial coverage (diagnostics yes, IVF procedure no, or medication only).
  4. No coverage, full self-pay.

## State IVF Mandate, Where You Live Matters
- Targets secondary kw: "state IVF mandate"
- 25 states + DC have some fertility mandate; coverage and cycle caps vary widely. `<Cite n="3" />` (Resolve fact sheet)
- Mandate ≠ guarantee: self-insured (ERISA) employer plans bypass state mandates.
- High-coverage examples: Massachusetts, New York, Illinois, New Jersey.
- `<Callout type="warning">` "Mandate to offer" vs "mandate to cover" is the make-or-break distinction.

## Employer Fertility Benefits, the Fastest-Growing Path to Coverage
- Targets secondary kw: "employer fertility benefits"
- Carve-out vendors (Progyny, Carrot, Maven, WIN) bypass traditional insurance with cycle-based or "smart cycle" allowances.
- Common benefit shapes: $25k-$75k lifetime, 1-4 cycles, donor + surrogacy coverage growing.
- Action step for the reader: HR portal → "fertility benefits" or "family building" → ask for the summary plan description.

## IVF Financing in the US, When Self-Pay Is the Only Path
- Targets secondary kw: "IVF financing US"
- Clinic-level: multi-cycle "shared risk" / refund programs (e.g., 3-cycle bundles with partial refund if no live birth).
- Third-party: Future Family, Sunfish, CapexMD, HFC Grants, Baby Quest.
- HSA/FSA dollars are eligible for IVF and medication.
- `<Callout type="note">` Shared-risk programs need careful math; they're insurance against bad outcomes, not always cheaper.

## NHS vs US Pathway (Quick Comparator)
- Two-column callout: NHS = eligibility lottery, taxpayer-funded if you qualify; US = insurance lottery, but employers increasingly cover.

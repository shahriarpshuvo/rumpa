## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Open with the question I hear most often in PCOS first appointments: "What should I do first when TTC with PCOS?" Reassure that most women with PCOS can and do conceive. PCOS and TTC tips work best when they are sequenced, not piled on at once.

## What PCOS Actually Does to Fertility ← targets: "PCOS lifestyle changes for conception"
- Irregular or absent ovulation is the bottleneck, not "PCOS makes you infertile"
- Insulin resistance drives high androgens and disrupted LH/FSH
- Eggs are usually fine; the timing of release is the issue
- `<Callout type="note">` PCOS is the leading cause of anovulatory infertility, not a verdict of infertility

## Month 1, Set the Foundations ← targets: "PCOS lifestyle changes for conception"
- Start a prenatal with 400 mcg folic acid (5 mg if BMI >30 or on metformin)
- Check vitamin D, thyroid, prolactin, HbA1c
- Sleep 7 to 9 hours, walk daily
- Reduce ultra-processed food, build meals around protein + fibre + healthy fat
- Modest 5-10% weight change improves ovulation in those with higher BMI; for lean PCOS, focus on resistance training and insulin sensitivity, not weight loss
- `<Cite n="1" />` ESHRE / NICE PCOS guidance

## Tracking Ovulation with PCOS, Why Standard Tools Mislead ← targets: "tracking ovulation with PCOS"
- LH baseline can be chronically elevated → false-positive OPKs
- BBT confirms ovulation has happened but does not predict it
- Cervical mucus tracking is often the most useful signal
- Pair: serial LH testing across many days, plus BBT, plus mucus
- Consider a mid-luteal progesterone (day 21 of a 28-day cycle, or 7 days before next expected period) to confirm ovulation
- Time intercourse across the fertile window, not on a single peak day
- `<Cite n="2" />` ACOG patient resource on ovulation tracking

## Supplements That Have Evidence ← targets: "PCOS supplements TTC"
- Myo-inositol 2 g twice daily (often with D-chiro-inositol at 40:1 ratio)
- Vitamin D, replete if deficient
- Omega-3 (DHA/EPA)
- Folate
- N-acetylcysteine in selected cases under medical supervision
- Avoid: blanket "PCOS detox" kits, seed cycling, anything claiming to "cure" PCOS
- `<Callout type="warning">` Supplements are adjuncts, not substitutes for diagnosis or ovulation induction
- `<Cite n="3" />` Inositol meta-analyses, see also [Inositol vs Metformin for PCOS Fertility](/blog/inositol-vs-metformin-pcos)

## When to Stop "Trying Naturally" and Ask for Help ← targets: "what should I do first when TTC with PCOS"
- If cycles are >35 days or absent, do not wait 12 months
- See a GP/OB-GYN at 6 months if <35, sooner if >35 or with anovulatory cycles
- Letrozole is first line for ovulation induction in PCOS in most guidelines
- Metformin is helpful in insulin-resistant phenotypes
- UK NHS pathway: GP referral after 12 months (or 6 if >35); US: self-refer to REI
- `<Cite n="4" />` NICE NG257 fertility pathway

## A Realistic Timeline, What Actually Helps You Conceive with PCOS ← bridges to Rumpa post / NHS-US pathway
- Months 0-3: lifestyle, supplements, baseline bloods
- Months 3-6: cycle tracking, partner semen analysis, GP review
- Months 6-12: ovulation induction (letrozole ± metformin) under specialist care
- Beyond: IUI or IVF, see [PCOS Fertility Treatment Options](/blog/pcos-fertility-treatment-options)
- For diaspora readers: late-marriage timing or family pressure does not change biology; an early baseline workup at age 30+ is sensible regardless of "how long you have been trying"

## What This Means for You (NOT "Conclusion")
- PCOS and TTC tips are most useful when they are sequenced and matched to your phenotype. Start with one cycle of foundation work, layer in cycle tracking and evidence-backed supplements, then escalate to medication early if cycles are not ovulatory. Soft `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`.

## What ACOG Recommends Before Pregnancy ← targets: "ACOG pre-conception screening"
- Key points:
  - ACOG Committee Opinion 762 frames pre-pregnancy counselling as the foundation of healthy pregnancy.
  - Core ACOG-recommended labs: CBC, blood type and antibody screen, rubella and varicella immunity, hepatitis B, HIV, syphilis, chlamydia screening where indicated, urinalysis.
  - Cervical cancer screening current.
  - Diabetes screening if at risk (BMI, family history, prior GDM, PCOS).
  - Long-tail answered in prose: "What labs should I get before pregnancy in the US?"
  - `<Cite n="1" />` to ACOG CO 762.

## Thyroid and Vitamin D, the Often-Skipped Pair ← targets: "TSH and vitamin D pre-pregnancy"
- Key points:
  - TSH screening: routine for symptomatic or high-risk women; many clinicians offer it pre-conception regardless.
  - Optimal pre-conception TSH targets and why "in range" may not be enough.
  - Vitamin D deficiency is widespread; insufficiency may affect pregnancy outcomes per emerging data.
  - `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` on requesting TSH if your OB doesn't routinely order it.

## Carrier Screening, the Genetic Question ← targets: "carrier screening US"
- Key points:
  - ACOG recommends offering carrier screening to all planning pregnancy.
  - Pan-ethnic baseline: spinal muscular atrophy, cystic fibrosis, hemoglobinopathies and thalassemias.
  - Expanded panels available; pros and cons.
  - Partner testing logic.
  - `<Cite n="2" />` to ACOG carrier screening guidance.

## Diabetes Risk Screening Before Pregnancy ← targets: secondary
- Key points:
  - HbA1c or fasting glucose if risk factors: obesity, PCOS, prior GDM, family history, certain ethnicities.
  - Bridge to PCOS content for women with PCOS-related glucose risk.
  - Internal link to pcos-and-gd-link.

## What Insurance Usually Covers ← targets: "what does insurance cover for pre-pregnancy bloods"
- Key points:
  - Under the ACA, many preventive women's health services are covered without cost share at in-network providers; this includes well-woman visits and certain screenings.
  - Carrier screening coverage varies dramatically by plan and state.
  - Out-of-pocket reality for self-pay or high-deductible plans.
  - Patient script: how to ask your OB and your insurer about coverage.
  - `<Callout type="note">` framing on the US insurance reality without partisan tone.

## Special Cases, When You Need More Than the Basics ← targets: long-tail
- Key points:
  - PCOS: OGTT/fasting glucose/HbA1c per 2023 International PCOS Guideline.
  - Recurrent miscarriage (2+): antiphospholipid syndrome, thyroid antibodies, karyotype, structural workup.
  - Age 35+: AMH and antral follicle count discussion (fertility assessment, not pregnancy safety).
  - Chronic conditions: thyroid, lupus, diabetes, hypertension; meds review pre-conception.
  - Internal link to ttc-after-35.

## How to Get the Right Tests at Your Visit ← targets: bridge
- Key points:
  - Book a pre-conception visit explicitly, not just an annual well-woman.
  - Bring family history, current meds, and supplement list.
  - A sample question list ("What's my TSH? Should I get carrier screening? Am I rubella immune?").
  - Bridge link to pre-pregnancy-bloods-uk for UK readers or comparison.

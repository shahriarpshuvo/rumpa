## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Open with the reader's quiet maths: "If you've just started trying, or you're four months in and counting cycles, you're probably asking how long does it take to get pregnant on average." Acknowledge that the answer is more nuanced than "85% within a year". Primary keyword in first 100 words.

## The Short Answer by Cycle ← targets: "average time to conceive"
- For healthy couples under 35: roughly 15-20% chance per cycle, 30-40% cumulative by 3 months, 70-75% by 6 months, 85% or more by 12 months.
- For under-40 NICE-defined cohort: over 80% conceive within one year, rising to over 90% in the second year.
- `<Cite n="1" />` NICE CG156 / NG257.
- `<Callout type="note">` "Bold takeaway: most pregnancies happen within the first 6 cycles. The 7th cycle is not failure."

## What "Average" Hides (the Curve Is Not Flat) ← targets: "cumulative pregnancy rate"
- Per-cycle probability stays roughly constant for the fertile fraction of a cohort; what changes is who is left in the cohort each month.
- Some couples conceive in cycle 1 not because they're "lucky" but because their per-cycle chance is at the top of the range.
- Couples still trying at cycle 8 often have a lower (but not zero) per-cycle chance; this is when basic testing becomes worth considering.
- `<Cite n="2" />` PubMed cumulative pregnancy study.

## How Age Changes the Timeline ← targets: "age and fertility"
- Under 30: average per-cycle probability around 20-25%, ~84-88% pregnant within 12 cycles.
- 30 to 34: cumulative ~88% within a year.
- 35 to 39: ~46% within 6 cycles, ~67% within 12 cycles.
- 40+: per-cycle chance drops to roughly 5% (ASRM).
- Long-tail prose: patients ask "what is the average time to conceive at 30?", and the honest answer is most couples in their early 30s conceive within 6 to 12 cycles of well-timed intercourse.
- `<Cite n="3" />` ASRM age and fertility committee opinion.

## When to Worry, and When Not To ← targets: long-tail "how many cycles before I should worry"
- NICE / NHS evaluation thresholds: 12 months under 35, 6 months at 35 or older, sooner if known risk (irregular cycles, prior pelvic infection, endometriosis, prior chemo, male partner risk factors).
- ASRM (US) uses the same 12/6 threshold but in practice many US private clinics will evaluate sooner if requested.
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` "Patients who come to me in cycle 4 worried they've waited too long, I reassure them that month 4 is statistically still early. Patients in cycle 9 with irregular cycles, I move them forward."
- Bridge to `ttc-6-months-no-pregnancy` and `when-to-see-fertility-specialist`.

## Things That Shift the Curve (in Either Direction) ← bridges to other Dr. Rumpa posts
- Faster: timed intercourse around ovulation, healthy BMI, no smoking, optimised partner sperm health, treated thyroid.
- Slower: irregular cycles (PCOS / PMOS), male factor, age over 35, prior pelvic surgery, untreated thyroid disease, very high or very low BMI, smoking.

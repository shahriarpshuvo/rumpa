## Why fasting glucose is the hardest GD number to move ← targets: "fasting glucose pregnancy"
- Brief physiology: dawn phenomenon, overnight liver glucose release, placental hormone interference
- Why dinner choices alone often cannot fix it
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` A high morning reading is rarely a "willpower" issue, it's hormones
- `<Cite n="1" />` ADA Standards of Care in Pregnancy, NICE NG3

## What makes a bedtime snack safe with GD ← targets: "bedtime snack carb-protein pairing"
- 15-30g complex carb plus 7-15g protein, ideally some fat
- Timing: 30-60 minutes before sleep
- Avoid: fruit alone, fruit juice, white crackers, sugary yogurt
- Plain English on why pairing slows absorption (gastric emptying, insulin response curve)

## Safe bedtime snack examples that follow the rule ← targets: "gestational diabetes safe snacks bedtime"
- "What to eat before bed with gestational diabetes" answered in prose with categories not just a list
- 6-8 examples: Greek yoghurt with berries, wholegrain toast with nut butter, hummus with veg and oatcakes, cheese and apple, hard-boiled egg with oatcake, milk and a small handful of nuts, cottage cheese with seeds, hummus with wholegrain crackers
- `<Callout type="note">` Test, learn, repeat: your body's snack response is more reliable than any list

## Why your fasting sugar may still be high ← targets: "high morning blood sugar GD"
- "Why is my fasting sugar high with GD?" answered in prose
- Three common reasons: snack too small, snack too sweet, dawn phenomenon dominates
- Sleep position, stress, illness, and a missed late evening walk all matter
- When the pattern is genuinely hormone-driven, not diet-driven

## When snacks are not enough, the medication conversation
- Threshold thinking: most NHS clinics start medication if 30% of morning readings are above target across one to two weeks
- Brief mention of metformin and insulin (linked out to deeper post)
- Bridge to internal post: insulin vs metformin for GD
- `<Callout type="warning">` Needing medication is not a personal failure, the placenta is the strongest variable

## What This Means for You
- Safe bedtime snacks for gestational diabetes are one of the few tools you control, but they have limits. Pair a complex carb with protein, eat 30 to 60 minutes before bed, log the morning reading, and adjust. If fasting numbers stay high after a week of consistent snacks, that's a clinical conversation to have without guilt. `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`

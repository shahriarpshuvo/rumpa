## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- A PCOS diet to conceive is not a strict food list, it is an eating pattern that lowers insulin spikes and supports ovulation. The 2023 International PCOS Guideline puts lifestyle first, before medication, for women with PCOS who are trying to conceive. This post sets out what to put on the plate, what to limit, and what a realistic day looks like.

## Why diet matters for PCOS fertility ← targets: "PCOS fertility nutrition"
- The insulin-androgen-anovulation loop in one paragraph
- Trial signal: a randomised study showed 24.6 percent ovulatory cycles on a low-GI diet vs 7.4 percent on a normal-GI diet over 3 months
- Eggs take roughly 90 days to mature, dietary change shows up in cycles over weeks to months, not days
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Diet is a lever, not a cure, pair it with the right testing and, where indicated, medication
- `<Cite n="1" />` 2023 International Evidence-based PCOS Guideline

## What "low-GI eating PCOS" actually means in real meals ← targets: "low-GI eating PCOS"
- Glycaemic index in plain English (how fast a food lifts blood glucose)
- Protein plus fibre plus healthy fat with every meal flattens the curve
- Whole grains over refined (oats, basmati rice, wholewheat roti over white bread, white rice)
- Pulses, beans, and lentils as anchor proteins, low-GI plus high-fibre
- Berries and apples with skin, non-starchy vegetables (spinach, broccoli, peppers, okra)
- Oily fish 2 to 3 times a week (salmon, sardines, mackerel) for omega-3
- `<Cite n="2" />` British Dietetic Association PCOS food fact sheet

## Foods to avoid with PCOS ← targets: "foods to avoid with PCOS"
- Sugar-sweetened drinks (the single biggest insulin-spike lever for most people)
- Refined carbs eaten alone (white bread, sugary cereals, white rice, plain pasta)
- Trans fats in fried foods and packaged bakery items
- Heavily processed snack foods
- Alcohol while TTC (per NHS pre-pregnancy guidance)
- `<Callout type="note">` "Avoid" does not mean "never", aim for everyday patterns, not perfection

## A sample PCOS fertility meal plan day ← targets: "sample PCOS fertility meal plan day"
- Breakfast: Greek yoghurt with berries, chia, walnuts, cinnamon (or savoury option: eggs with spinach on wholegrain toast)
- Mid-morning: apple with a small handful of almonds
- Lunch: lentil and spinach dal, brown basmati rice, salad with olive oil
- Afternoon: hummus with carrot and cucumber sticks
- Dinner: grilled salmon, roasted vegetables, quinoa or sweet potato
- Evening if hungry: small bowl of berries with full-fat yoghurt
- "What to eat to ovulate with PCOS" answered in prose around this plate
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` South Asian readers, swap basmati for wholewheat roti, replace dal with rajma or chana, the pattern is what matters

## Supplements that earn their place
- Folic acid 400 mcg (5 mg if BMI over 30, diabetes, neural tube history)
- Vitamin D 10 mcg (UK default, check level if you do not get sun)
- Omega-3 from food first, supplement if you do not eat oily fish
- Inositol (myo plus D-chiro 40:1): modest but reasonable evidence in PCOS ovulation
- Bridge to Rumpa post on inositol vs metformin

## What This Means for You
- A PCOS diet to conceive is steady and unglamorous: low-GI carbs, protein and fibre with every meal, oily fish, fewer sugary drinks, a folic acid and vitamin D base.

## The Short Answer, You Didn't Cause This ← targets: "is gestational diabetes my fault"
- Key points:
  - Clear answer first, then the explanation.
  - GD develops in 4-10% of pregnancies globally, with rates higher in South Asian, Black, and East Asian populations.
  - Even women with no risk factors can develop GD; women with every risk factor sometimes don't.
  - `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` with the patient-facing reassurance.

## What Actually Drives Gestational Diabetes ← targets: "placental hormones insulin resistance"
- Key points:
  - The placenta secretes hormones (human placental lactogen, cortisol, progesterone, oestrogen) that intentionally make the mother more insulin resistant, to push glucose to the baby.
  - In most pregnancies the pancreas keeps up; in GD pregnancies it cannot meet the new demand.
  - Answer the long-tail "what really causes gestational diabetes" inline.
  - `<Cite n="1" />` to a recent GDM pathophysiology review.

## Risk Factors That Raise the Odds (Not Cause) ← targets: "gestational diabetes risk factors"
- Key points:
  - Family history of type 2 diabetes
  - South Asian, Black, Middle Eastern, Hispanic ethnicity (per NICE NG3 and ACOG)
  - Pre-pregnancy BMI over 30
  - PCOS / PMOS
  - Previous baby over 4.5 kg or previous GD
  - Age over 40
  - Clarify: risk factor does not equal cause. The biology above is the cause.

## The Things You Could Not Have Changed ← targets: "GD guilt and mental health"
- Key points:
  - Genetics, ethnicity, family history, age, prior GD: not modifiable.
  - Even diet-perfect, exercise-perfect women get GD; this is well documented.
  - Address the guilt cycle directly. Internal link to mental-wellbeing pillar planned later in calendar.
  - `<Callout type="note">` validating the emotion, redirecting to action.

## What You Can Do Now (Not What You Should Have Done) ← targets: long-tail forward-looking
- Key points:
  - Forward-focused: glucose monitoring, dietary changes, gentle movement, medication if needed.
  - Reassure that well-controlled GD has outcomes comparable to non-GD pregnancies.
  - Postnatal: 6-week OGTT, lifelong type 2 risk screening.

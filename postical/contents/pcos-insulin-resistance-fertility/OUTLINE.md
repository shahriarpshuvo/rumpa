## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- One question keeps coming up in PCOS clinics: why am I not ovulating when my hormones seem treatable? The PCOS insulin resistance fertility link is the answer for most women, around 65 to 80 percent of women with PCOS have some degree of insulin resistance, and that single issue often drives the irregular cycles. This post explains how the chain works and what to test for.

## How insulin resistance drives PCOS infertility ← targets: "insulin and ovulation"
- Plain-English mechanism: cells respond less to insulin, the pancreas pumps out more, hyperinsulinaemia signals the ovary to make more androgens
- Excess androgens disrupt follicle selection, the dominant follicle does not emerge, ovulation stalls
- Long-tail in prose: "how does insulin resistance stop ovulation" answered here
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` This is why PCOS responds better to insulin-targeted treatment than to oestrogen-only fixes
- `<Cite n="1" />` 2023 International Evidence-based PCOS Guideline

## How to test for insulin resistance with PCOS ← targets: "HOMA-IR test"
- Fasting insulin plus fasting glucose, calculate HOMA-IR (fasting insulin in microIU/mL multiplied by fasting glucose in mmol/L, divided by 22.5)
- Common research cutoff for insulin resistance: HOMA-IR at or above 2.71 (used in IVF pre-treatment studies)
- HbA1c picks up established dysglycaemia, not early insulin resistance
- Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) with insulin levels is the more sensitive option, often the right test in lean PCOS
- "How to test for insulin resistance with PCOS" answered in prose with UK + US ordering pathways
- `<Callout type="note">` Many GPs do not order fasting insulin by default, you may need to ask for it specifically
- `<Cite n="2" />` Frontiers Endocrinology 2023 HOMA-IR in PCOS IVF

## What insulin and ovulation look like in real cycles
- Anovulatory cycles, long cycles over 35 days, very heavy bleeds after a long gap
- Why OPKs misfire in PCOS (chronically elevated baseline LH)
- BBT often shows no clear shift when ovulation does not happen
- Bridge to Rumpa post on PCOS cycle tracking

## Metformin for insulin resistance and fertility ← targets: "metformin for insulin resistance"
- Mechanism: improves insulin sensitivity, lowers hyperinsulinaemia, modestly improves ovulation rates
- Dose ramp up (usually 500 mg titrated to 1500 to 2000 mg daily), side effect window of the first 2 to 4 weeks
- Evidence summary: metformin alone is less effective than letrozole or clomiphene for ovulation induction, but combination therapy helps clomiphene-resistant PCOS
- Continued in pregnancy in some protocols, decided case-by-case
- `<Callout type="warning">` Metformin is not a weight loss drug, in PCOS it is an insulin sensitiser, framing it as a diet pill misses the point
- `<Cite n="3" />` 2023 PCOS Guideline on pharmacotherapy
- `<Cite n="4" />` Update on therapeutic role of metformin in PCOS, 2025 review

## Lifestyle levers that actually shift insulin resistance
- Strength training plus regular movement, not just cardio
- Low-glycaemic eating pattern, protein and fibre with every meal
- Sleep (under-discussed): short sleep worsens insulin resistance independent of diet
- Inositol (myo plus D-chiro 40:1 ratio) as a complementary option, evidence is modest but reasonable
- Bridge to Rumpa posts on PCOS diet to conceive and inositol vs metformin

## What This Means for You
- The PCOS insulin resistance fertility loop is treatable, often without jumping straight to IVF. Ask for fasting insulin and glucose to calculate HOMA-IR, address sleep and movement, and talk to your doctor about whether metformin or inositol fits your numbers.

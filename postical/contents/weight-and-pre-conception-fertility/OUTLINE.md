## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- The link between weight and pre-conception fertility is real but more nuanced than headlines suggest. Both ends of the BMI scale can disrupt ovulation, and small changes often matter more than the number on the scale. Here is what I tell patients in clinic when weight comes up during a TTC visit.

## How Weight Talks to Your Ovaries ← targets: "BMI and ovulation"
- Adipose tissue produces estrogen; too much OR too little disrupts the HPO axis
- Leptin signalling and energy availability
- Insulin resistance pathway (separate spoke for PCOS-specific)
- `<Cite n="1" />` ASRM Obesity and Reproduction committee opinion (2021)

## What the BMI Numbers Actually Mean ← targets: "weight and fertility pre-conception"
- BMI ≤ 18.5: underweight; ≥ 25 overweight; ≥ 30 obese (WHO classification)
- Why BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnosis
- South Asian patients: ethnicity-adjusted BMI cut-offs (NICE notes lower thresholds)
- `<Callout type="note">` BMI alone never tells the full fertility story

## Underweight and Fertility ← targets: "underweight and infertility"
- Hypothalamic amenorrhoea: when energy availability is too low to ovulate
- Athletes, restrictive eating histories, chronic stress
- Long-tail in prose: "can being underweight stop ovulation"
- ASRM: BMI < 19 associated with up to 4x longer time to conceive
- Restoring cycles: refeeding, reducing exercise load, addressing eating disorder history with a specialist
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Patients who come to me with absent periods and low BMI, I help them understand the energy-availability piece first

## Overweight, Obesity, and Conception ← targets: "BMI and ovulation"
- Anovulation, longer time to pregnancy, lower live birth rates with treatment
- PCOS interaction (link to PCOS cluster)
- Miscarriage risk acknowledged honestly, without panic
- `<Cite n="2" />` ESHRE/ACOG pre-conception counselling guidance

## Why Modest Loss (5-10%) Matters ← targets: "modest weight loss fertility benefit"
- 5-10% body-weight reduction can restore ovulation in many anovulatory women
- Long-tail in prose: "does losing weight help you conceive"
- Why "modest" is the operative word, not extreme dieting
- `<Callout type="warning">` Crash diets are counter-productive; sustained, modest, supported change works

## Practical Pre-Conception Steps (No Diet Talk) ← targets: "weight and fertility pre-conception"
- Movement that you enjoy and can sustain
- Sleep and stress, both undervalued
- Nutrition density over calorie counts
- When to involve a registered dietitian or endocrinologist
- South Asian context: family meals, ghee-and-rice traditions, no shame framing

## What This Means for You
- Weight and pre-conception fertility deserve an honest conversation, not a number-shaming one. Whether you sit above or below the "ideal" BMI, small sustained shifts can meaningfully change your chances. If your cycles are irregular, talk to your doctor about a fertility check. `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`

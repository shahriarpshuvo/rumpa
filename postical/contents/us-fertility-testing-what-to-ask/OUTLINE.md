## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Walking into a fertility consult in the US is unlike anywhere else: you are paying for the visit, the labs, the imaging, and often the negotiation with your insurer. Knowing what fertility testing in the US to ask your doctor about (and what to ask your plan) protects both your timeline and your wallet. This is the question script I share with patients before their first US fertility appointment.

## Why the US Fertility Workup Looks Different ← targets: "fertility testing US what to ask doctor"
- Fee-for-service model: every test billed separately
- State mandates patchwork (RESOLVE map), 2026 California SB 729 expansion
- AMH testing US is sometimes self-pay even when ordered by OB-GYN
- `<Callout type="note">` Coverage definitions of "infertility" differ by insurer

## The 6 Core Tests You Should Expect ← targets: "what tests will an OB-GYN run for infertility"
- Day-3 hormones: FSH, LH, estradiol
- AMH (ovarian reserve)
- TSH + prolactin
- Pelvic ultrasound + antral follicle count
- HSG (hysterosalpingogram) for tubal patency
- Semen analysis for the partner
- `<Cite n="1" />` ASRM Fertility Evaluation Committee Opinion (2021)

## Questions to Ask Your OB-GYN or REI ← targets: "fertility specialist questions"
- Which tests are appropriate at my age and history?
- Are you ordering AMH testing US-wide reference ranges or lab-specific cut-offs?
- What is your timeline from workup to treatment decision?
- Will you do the workup or refer me to a reproductive endocrinologist (REI)?
- "What questions should I ask my fertility doctor about lifestyle changes before treatment?" framed in prose
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` First-person: in clinic, I encourage patients to bring this list printed out

## Questions to Ask Your Insurance Plan ← targets: "insurance coverage fertility tests"
- How does my plan define infertility (some require 12 months trying)?
- Are diagnostic codes vs treatment codes covered differently?
- What is my deductible reset date?
- Is AMH covered when billed with an infertility diagnosis code?
- Pre-authorization for HSG and saline sonogram?
- `<Cite n="2" />` RESOLVE state coverage map

## What Results Mean (Plain English) ← targets: "AMH testing US"
- AMH ranges interpreted by age, not absolute number
- Day-3 FSH > 10 IU/L vs egg-reserve picture
- Antral follicle count: 5-10 is the typical concern threshold
- When "normal" results still warrant a specialist referral
- `<Callout type="warning">` Single numbers do not equal a diagnosis

## Red Flags Worth a Second Opinion ← targets: "fertility specialist questions"
- Pressure to start IVF before basic workup is complete
- No tubal patency test offered
- Partner not tested
- Quote that excludes monitoring or anesthesia from IVF estimate

## What This Means for You
- Fertility testing in the US is navigable when you arrive prepared. Bring this question script to your OB-GYN and a separate one to your insurer. Two phone calls before your appointment can save thousands. `<ConsultCTA placement="end" />`

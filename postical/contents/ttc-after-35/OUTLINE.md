## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Many women come to clinic asking the same thing: is it too late to start trying? TTC after 35 advice should be honest, not panicky, the per-cycle chance of conception does fall, but it falls on a slope, not a cliff. This post sets out what changes biologically, what tests to ask for, and when to push for a specialist referral.

## What actually changes after 35 ← targets: "advanced maternal age fertility"
- Per-cycle conception data: roughly 25 percent at 35, dropping toward 10 percent at 40 (ACOG, ASRM)
- Cumulative 12-month conception still 66 to 75 percent at 35
- Miscarriage rises (about 20 percent in early-to-mid 30s, around 33 percent at 40 to 44)
- `<Callout type="note">` "Falls off a cliff at 35" is a media line, the curve is a slope until 38, then steeper
- `<Cite n="1" />` ACOG FAQ Having a Baby After 35
- `<Cite n="2" />` StatPearls Age-Related Fertility Decline

## Egg quality after 35, the part nobody explains well ← targets: "egg quality after 35"
- Aneuploidy rates rise with age, this drives both lower fertilisation success and higher early loss
- AMH measures quantity, not quality, both matter and they decline at different rates
- Antral follicle count, what your scan can and cannot predict
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` AMH does not predict natural fertility in any given month, it helps plan if and when to escalate
- `<Cite n="3" />` NICE NG257 Fertility problems guideline

## A realistic 6-month TTC plan in your late 30s ← targets: "TTC after 35 advice"
- Time intercourse to the fertile window every cycle (LH OPKs are still the most cost-effective starting tool)
- Folic acid 400 mcg from before conception, 5 mg if BMI over 30, diabetes, or family history of neural tube defects
- Partner sperm analysis at month 1, not month 6 (saves the most time when age is the variable)
- Address modifiable factors: smoking, alcohol, BMI band, thyroid, vitamin D
- Bridge to Rumpa post on signs of ovulation and the fertile window calculator

## What tests to ask for if TTC after 35 ← targets: "what tests should I get if TTC after 35"
- Day 2 to 5 FSH, LH, oestradiol
- AMH (any day of cycle)
- TSH and free T4
- Prolactin
- HbA1c and ferritin
- Pelvic ultrasound for antral follicle count and uterine anatomy
- Tubal patency test (HSG or HyCoSy) if TTC for 3 to 6 months without success
- Partner semen analysis early
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Bring these as a written list to your GP or OB-GYN appointment, it shortens the conversation

## When to escalate to a specialist after 35 ← targets: "when to escalate to specialist after 35"
- UK pathway: NICE recommends earlier referral if the woman is 36 or older, after 6 months of regular unprotected intercourse without conception
- US pathway: ACOG and ASRM recommend evaluation after 6 months at 35 plus, after 3 months at 40 plus
- "How quickly should I see a doctor if I am 38 and TTC" answered in prose: sooner rather than later, ideally by month 3
- Red flags that mean go now, not in 6 months: irregular or absent periods, previous pelvic surgery, known endometriosis, partner with prior fertility concerns
- `<Cite n="4" />` HFEA on age and treatment access

## What This Means for You
- TTC after 35 advice that helps is specific, not scary. Track your fertile window, get the basic bloods early, ask your partner to do a semen analysis in month 1, and book a fertility review at 6 months (sooner if you are 38 plus or your cycles are irregular).

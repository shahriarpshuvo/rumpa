## Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Couples often come to clinic with the same question: should we try IUI first or jump to IVF? Asking "IUI vs IVF which is better" misses the better question, which is what fits your diagnosis, your age, and your budget. This post lays out both treatments side by side and the evidence on when each one earns its place.

## IUI vs IVF in one paragraph each ← targets: "IUI vs IVF which is better"
- IUI (intrauterine insemination): washed sperm placed directly into the uterus around ovulation, sometimes with mild ovarian stimulation
- IVF (in vitro fertilisation): ovarian stimulation, egg retrieval, fertilisation in the lab, embryo transfer back to the uterus
- The fundamental difference: IUI helps sperm find an egg, IVF makes the embryo in the lab and bypasses tubes
- `<Cite n="1" />` SART overview of IUI vs IVF
- `<Cite n="2" />` HFEA on IUI

## IUI success rate, the honest numbers ← targets: "IUI success rate"
- Per-cycle pregnancy: roughly 10 to 20 percent for women under 35, dropping toward 5 percent or less at 40 plus
- Cumulative over 3 to 4 cycles: 30 to 45 percent for younger women with good-quality sperm and at least one open tube
- Mild male factor and unexplained infertility are the strongest IUI candidates
- `<Callout type="note">` IUI success drops sharply with age and with severe sperm or tubal issues, going straight to IVF can save time
- `<Cite n="3" />` NICE NG257 on assisted conception

## IVF success rate and cost comparison ← targets: "IVF cost comparison"
- Per-cycle pregnancy: 40 to 50 percent under 35, around 32 percent at 35 to 37, around 10 percent at 40 plus (HFEA UK and SART US data)
- UK costs: NHS funded where eligible (3 cycles under 40 per NICE, postcode lottery in practice), private around 5,000 to 8,000 pounds per cycle excluding medication
- US costs: 12,000 to 20,000 dollars per cycle, partial insurance in some states
- IUI costs by contrast: 300 to 1,000 dollars in the US, around 800 to 1,500 pounds per cycle privately in the UK
- Cumulative IUI costs can approach half of one IVF cycle, this matters in the decision
- `<Cite n="4" />` HFEA fertility treatment 2024 trends
- Bridge to Rumpa posts on IVF cost UK and IVF cost US

## Unexplained infertility IUI vs IVF ← targets: "unexplained infertility IUI vs IVF"
- FASTT trial finding: skipping the second round of IUI and moving to IVF resulted in faster time to pregnancy and fewer total cycles
- For unexplained infertility under 35, 2 to 3 medicated IUI cycles is a common starting point before IVF
- For women 38 plus with unexplained infertility, many specialists go straight to IVF
- Long-tail in prose: "when does IUI fail and IVF make sense" answered with a decision rule

## A decision matrix by diagnosis
- Mild male factor: start with IUI, move to IVF with ICSI if 3 cycles fail
- Unexplained infertility under 35: 2 to 3 IUI cycles then IVF
- PCOS with patent tubes: ovulation induction first, then IUI, then IVF if no response
- Blocked or absent tubes: IVF is the appropriate first step, IUI bypasses nothing useful
- Severe male factor (very low count or motility): IVF with ICSI from the start
- Age 38 plus with reduced ovarian reserve: IVF earlier to make the most of remaining eggs
- `<Callout type="clinical-tip">` Diagnosis drives the answer, age accelerates it, your specialist should map both before recommending a path

## How many rounds of IUI before IVF ← targets: "how many rounds of IUI before IVF"
- 3 to 4 medicated IUI cycles is the standard ceiling, after which IVF gives the better return per attempt
- Some pathways switch to IVF after 2 cycles in women 38 plus
- Diminishing returns are real, success per cycle drops sharply after attempt 4
- `<Cite n="5" />` Practice committee guidance on cycle limits

## What This Means for You
- IUI vs IVF which is better depends on diagnosis, age, and how much time and money the cycles cost you. IUI is the right first step for mild male factor, unexplained infertility under 35, and PCOS with open tubes. IVF earns its place when tubes are blocked, sperm is severely affected, age is 38 plus, or 3 IUI cycles have not worked.

## Tier 1 — Doctor-Led Competitors

### Dr. Natalie Crawford, MD (Fora Fertility)

#### Overview
- **URL:** https://www.nataliecrawfordmd.com
- **Description:** Board-certified OB-GYN and reproductive endocrinologist (REI); founder of Fora Fertility; author of *The Fertility Formula*. Personal infertility/pregnancy-loss story drives the brand.
- **Content Strategy:** Hub-and-spoke — podcast (*As a Woman*) as primary content engine, blog supports each episode, Instagram + TikTok daily, book as authority anchor. Science-backed, patient-empathetic, first-person doctor voice. Strong PCOS coverage, IVF education, TTC fundamentals.
- **Strengths:** Highest-trust positioning; book + podcast = strongest authority moat in this niche; explicit "fertility education" brand
- **Gaps Dr. Rumpa can exploit:** US-centric (no NHS pathway content), limited gestational diabetes coverage, no South Asian cultural lens

### Dr. Lora Shahine, MD, FACOG (Pacific NW Fertility)

#### Overview
- **URL:** https://drlorashahine.com
- **Description:** Double board-certified REI + OB-GYN in Seattle; reaches 3M+ people/month; podcast *Baby or Bust*.
- **Content Strategy:** Multi-platform omnipresence — YouTube + Instagram + TikTok + podcast + blog. Specialism: miscarriage + recurrent pregnancy loss + environmental toxins. Educational, stigma-shattering tone.
- **Strengths:** Highest reach in niche (3M/mo); miscarriage authority is differentiating
- **Gaps Dr. Rumpa can exploit:** Heavy miscarriage focus leaves room on "first-time TTC" and "PCOS-first TTC" content; no UK/global angle; no South Asian diaspora lens

## Cross-Competitor Insights

### What works in this niche (do this)
- **First-person clinical experience** — "In my clinic, I see…" framing (Crawford, Shahine model)
- **Personal patient/doctor story** — own-infertility narrative builds trust (Crawford)
- **Multi-platform repurposing** — podcast/video as anchor, blog as long-tail, IG/TT for top-funnel reach (all three doctor competitors)
- **Authority anchors** — book, podcast, TV slot signal credibility (Crawford book, Corda book + ITV)
- **Stigma-breaking, empathetic tone** — patients respond to honest miscarriage/infertility talk (Shahine)
- **Patient-language explainers** — translate jargon (LH surge, anti-Müllerian hormone, etc.) into plain English with visual aids

### What to avoid (don't do this)
- **Holistic/woo overreach** — Corda gets flak from evidence-purists; Dr. Rumpa should stay clearly evidence-based
- **Pure app-funnel framing** — Premom-style "every article pushes a product" feels promotional; lead with education
- **Depersonalised editorial voice** — Flo's bland tone is the gap Dr. Rumpa fills
- **US-only framing** — leaves UK/global English-speaking patients underserved
- **Naturopathic / non-MD authority** — Dr. Rumpa's MD + practising OB-GYN status is the moat — lean into it

### Whitespace for Dr. Rumpa
1. **UK + US dual-market TTC content** — explicit NHS pathway + US insurance/IVF cost framing in same articles
2. **PCOS/PMOS as TTC pathway** — Crawford touches it; Premom does diet; nobody owns "PCOS protocol → conception" end-to-end from an OB-GYN
3. **South Asian cultural lens** — diaspora patients underserved; family/marriage timing/pressure context absent everywhere (no gender-pressure framing — see forbidden topics)
4. **TTC → GD continuum** — once a hard-won pregnancy starts, none of the doctor-competitors own gestational-diabetes content; Dr. Rumpa can keep the patient through pregnancy

### Forbidden topics (do NOT publish)
- **Sex selection / gender selection / "how to conceive a boy or girl"** — strictly off-limits across blog, IG, TikTok, consult marketing. No SEO play, no "myth-busting" angle, no cultural-context article. Reason: clinical ethics + brand-safety + platform-policy risk. If asked in DMs/comments, reply with a brief evidence-based decline and redirect to a healthy-conception topic.

### Reach & authority gap
- Crawford + Shahine combined reach ≈ 5M+/mo. Dr. Rumpa starts at zero. Strategy: don't compete on volume; compete on **niche depth + UK angle + South Asian fluency**. Two years of consistent daily content (per stated cadence) + book/podcast within 12 months = realistic authority play.
